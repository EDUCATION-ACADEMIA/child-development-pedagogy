CHILD DEVELOPMENT AND PEDAGOGY

[SOURCES]
Chunking is useful in the skill of ____

(A) Listening

(B) Speaking

(C) ** Reading

(D) Writing

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: –Chunking is a procedure of breaking up reading material into manageable sections. Before reading a “chunk” students are given a statement of purpose, which guides them to look for something specific in the text. This process is repeated until students complete the passage.

Concept note-2: -Chunking helps students identify key words and ideas, develops their ability to paraphrase, and makes it easier for them to organize and synthesize information.

Concept note-3: -The purpose of chunking is to maximize the utilization capacity of someone’s short-term memory. Because the capacity is between four and seven plus or minus two, chunking allows the individual to store more information in their short-term memory by assigning the information into groups.

Concept note-4: -One example is looking at the letters “an”. 2. They then learn to add a beginning or ending sound to make a new word, such as “can”, or “any”. For older students it may be looking at a word such as “bystander", and noting it is made up of “by” and “stand” with the suffix of “er”.

Concept note-5: -The APA Dictionary of Psychology defines “chunking” as “the process by which the mind divides large pieces of information into smaller units (chunks) that are easier to retain in short-term memory… one item in memory can stand for multiple other items”.
