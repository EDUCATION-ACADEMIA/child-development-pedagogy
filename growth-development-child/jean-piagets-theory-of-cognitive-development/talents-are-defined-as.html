JEAN PIAGETS THEORY OF COGNITIVE DEVELOPMENT

[SOURCES]
Talents are defined as

(A) the way you feel about something

(B) natural looks and a good smile

(C) ** natural strengths and abilities

(D) money and power

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -A strength is the ability to consistently provide near-perfect performance in a specific activity. Talents are naturally recurring patterns of thought, feeling, or behavior that can be productively applied.

Concept note-2: -Understanding the Concept This bodes with the actual definition of natural talent: “an innate or inborn gift for a specific activity, either allowing one to demonstrate some immediate skill without practice, or to gain skill rapidly with minimal practice.”

Concept note-3: -Some common synonyms of talent are aptitude, bent, faculty, genius, gift, and knack. While all these words mean “a special ability for doing something, ” talent suggests a marked natural ability that needs to be developed.

Concept note-4: -Talent is an exceptional natural ability, especially in a particular activity, such as music. Talent is often thought of as the kind of ability that comes without training-something that you’re born with. It is often contrasted with skill, which is an ability acquired and developed through practice.

Concept note-5: -So what is the difference? Talent is what you’ve been given. Ability is how you grow the talent you’ve been given.
