CONCEPT OF SOCIALISATION

[SOURCES]
Norms which are usually considered morally significant and whose violation is considered a serious matter.

(A) Taboo

(B) ** Mores

(C) Subcultures

(D) Cultural Relativity

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Mores (mor-ays) are norms that embody the moral views and principles of a group. Violating them can have serious consequences. The strongest mores are legally protected with laws or other formal norms. In the United States, for instance, murder is considered immoral, and it’s punishable by law (a formal norm).

Concept note-2: -A taboo is a very strong negative norm; it is a strict prohibition of behavior that society holds so strongly that violating it results in extreme disgust or expulsion from the group or society. Often times the violator of the taboo is considered unfit to live in that society.

Concept note-3: -Mores are moral norms, meaning they have an element of right or wrong. Some examples of mores include lying, stealing, gossiping, bullying, and trespassing.

Concept note-4: -Definition: Mores are moral norms. The term ‘more’ comes from ‘morality’. If you break a more, society will consider you to be immoral.

Concept note-5: -Mores (/ˈmɔːreɪz/, sometimes /ˈmɔːriːz/; from Latin mōrēs [ˈmoːreːs], plural form of singular mōs, meaning “manner, custom, usage, or habit") are social norms that are widely observed within a particular society or culture. Mores determine what is considered morally acceptable or unacceptable within any given culture.
