CONCEPT OF SOCIALISATION

[SOURCES]
Which term means adopting new norms, values, attitudes, and behaviors?

(A) ** Resocialization

(B) Maturation

(C) Anticipatory Socialization

(D) Social Development

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -If socialization is the lifelong process of learning the values and norms of a given society, then resocialization refers to undergoing this process again by drastically changing one’s values and beliefs. Typically, this occurs in a new environment where the old rules no longer apply.

Concept note-2: -Resocialization-the process in which people adopt new norms, values, attitudes and behaviors.

Concept note-3: -Resocialization or resocialisation (British English) is the process by which one’s sense of social values, beliefs, and norms are re-engineered. The process is deliberately carried out in-parent households and military boot camps through an intense social process that may take place in a total institution.

Concept note-4: -Resocialization: The process of adopting new norms, values, attitudes and behaviors.

Concept note-5: -Resocialization is explained as the process of being socialized again when, for example, old behaviors that were helpful in a previous role are removed because they are no longer of use. Resocialization is necessary when a person moves to a senior care center, goes to boarding school, or serves time in jail.
