CONCEPT OF SOCIALISATION

[SOURCES]
Every 20 seconds a child is arrested

(A) ** true

(B) false

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Yes, the police can arrest children if they believe they have committed a crime. Typically, police stations will have a child welfare protection officer (Section 107 of JJ Act 2015) and in each district and city, there will be at least one special juvenile police unit.

Concept note-2: -Court cases against your child If your child is under 10, they cannot be taken to court and charged with a criminal offence. However, once they are 10 or over, they are treated in the same way as any young person under 18 and will be dealt with by the Youth Justice System.

Concept note-3: -Child Safety Order If a child has committed an offence or broken a Local Child Curfew, they can be placed under the supervision of a youth offending team . This is called a Child Safety Order. The order normally lasts for up to 3 months, but in some cases it can last for up to 12 months.
