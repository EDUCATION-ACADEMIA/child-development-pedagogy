CONCEPT OF SOCIALISATION

[SOURCES]
____ is behavior expected of a status in relation to another social status.

(A) An attribution

(B) ** A role

(C) An obligation

(D) A right

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -role, in sociology, the behaviour expected of an individual who occupies a given social position or status. A role is a comprehensive pattern of behaviour that is socially recognized, providing a means of identifying and placing an individual in a society.

Concept note-2: -In all of the many social groups that we as individuals belong to, we have a status and a role to fulfill. Status is our relative social position within a group, while a role is the part our society expects us to play in a given status. For example, a man may have the status of father in his family.

Concept note-3: -Role: a set of behavioral expectations associated with a given status. Role expectation: a group’s or society’s definition of the way a specific role ought to be played.

Concept note-4: -A status is simply a position in society or in a group. A ‘role’ is the behavioral aspect of status. Statuses are occupied and roles are played. A role is the manner in which a given individual fulfills the obligations of a status and enjoys its privileges.

Concept note-5: -According to sociologists, status describes the position a person occupies in a particular setting. We all occupy several statuses and play the roles that may be associated with them. A role is the set of norms, values, behaviors, and personality characteristics attached to a status.
