THEORY OF MULTIPLE INTELLIGENCE GARDNER

[SOURCES]
Map reading is related to ____ intelligence.

(A) body-kinesthetic

(B) logical-mathematical

(C) ** visual-spatial

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Visual-Spatial Intelligence Read and write for enjoyment. Are good at putting puzzles together. Interpret pictures, graphs, and charts well. Enjoy drawing, painting, and the visual arts.

Concept note-2: -Visual-spatial intelligence has been defined as “the capacity to perceive the visual world accurately, and/or perform transformations and modifications on perceptions, construct mental representations of visual information and use the representation to perform activities” (Zimmerman & Dean, 2011).

Concept note-3: -People use visual-spatial processing skills for many tasks, from tying shoes to reading a map. People also need visual processing skills to make sense of letters and numbers. For example, look at “6” and “9” and “W” and “M.” They have different meanings depending on how they’re rotated on the page.

Concept note-4: -Spatial intelligence is the concept of being able to successfully perceive and derive insight from visual data. This cognitive process is known as an aptitude for understanding visual information in the real and abstract word as well as an innate ability to envision information.
