The Zone of Proximal Development is?

(A) The must a child can learn in a given day

(B) What the child already knows

(C) ** What can be done on own and what can not be done without assistance

(D) The intelligence level of the child is projected to be at a given point

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The zone of proximal development is an area of learning that occurs when a person is assisted by a teacher or peer with a higher skill set. The person learning the skill set cannot complete it without the assistance of the teacher or peer.

Concept note-2: -The Zone of Proximal Development is defined as the space between what a learner can do without assistance and what a learner can do with adult guidance or in collaboration with more capable peers.

Concept note-3: -To find the zone of proximal development, parents and teachers have to ask questions and observe a child’s skills. This will help them understand the skills that a child already knows, and based on this information, teachers can create a curriculum to advance those skills.

Concept note-4: -The zone of proximal development consists of two important components: the student’s potential development and the role of interaction with others. Learning occurs in the zone of proximal development after the identification of current knowledge.
