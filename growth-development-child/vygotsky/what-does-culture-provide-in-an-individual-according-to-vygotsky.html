What does culture provide in an individual, according to Vygotsky?

(A) Elements so they can live.

(B) Accumulated knowledge is passed on

(C) ** Processes of social development with which the processes of personal development are intertwined.

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Vygotsky believed that human activities take place in cultural settings and cannot be understood apart from the settings-our specific mental structures and processes can be traced to our interactions with others.

Concept note-2: -In the social development theory, Leo Vygotsky primarily explains that socialization affects the learning process in an individual. It tries to explain consciousness or awareness as the result of socialization. This means that when we talk to our peers or adults, we talk to them for the sake of communication.

Concept note-3: -Vygotsky’s sociocultural theory also suggested that children internalise and learn from the beliefs and attitudes that they witness around them. He believed that culture played an important role in shaping cognitive development and therefore that this development varied across cultures.

Concept note-4: -Vygotsky’s view is that social interaction plays a fundamental role in the development of cognition, or thinking. While the Socratic method of inquiry is frequently used in philosophy, it is also useful to create opportunities for self-reflection (Schön) and collaborative information creation.
