Is hiring cheaper than employee retention?

(A) ** False

(B) True

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The downsides to high employee retention are disengaged employees who remain in their roles, hurt productivity, create toxic work environments, and drive good employees away. High retention can also lead to difficulty implementing change, less innovation, and a lack of diversity and inclusion.

Concept note-2: -For some companies the cost of a bad hire can be as high as $240, 000. The average cost to hire an employee is $4, 425. It generally takes at least six months for companies to break even on new hires.
