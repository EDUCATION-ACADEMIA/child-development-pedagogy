It happens when the existing schema does not work and needs to be changed

(A) Cognition

(B) Schema

(C) Assimilation

(D) ** Accommodation

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Accommodation: Involves altering existing ideas or schemas as a result of new experiences. The process of accommodation involves altering one’s existing schemas, or ideas, as a result of new information or new experiences.

Concept note-2: -Assimilation describes how we interpret new experiences in terms of our current understanding, so in terms of our current schemas. Accommodation describes how we later adjust our schemas to better incorporate new experiences.

Concept note-3: -Accommodation – Another part of adaptation involves changing or altering our existing schemas in light of new information, a process known as accommodation.

Concept note-4: -Accommodation is when schemas are altered or new ones are formed as a result of new information or experiences. It is an adaptation process used to progress from one stage of cognitive development to another.

Concept note-5: -The processes through which schemas are adjusted or changed are known as assimilation and accommodation.
