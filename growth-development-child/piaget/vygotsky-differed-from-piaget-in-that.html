Vygotsky differed from Piaget in that:

(A) Piaget believed that environmental stimuli contributed to development.

(B) ** Vygotsky’s theory suggests that learning precedes development.

(C) Piaget spent his career in the Stalinist Soviet Union.

(D) Piaget believed that learning precedes development.

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Vygotsky argued that social learning preceded cognitive development. In other words, culture affects cognitive development. Whereas Piaget asserted that all children pass through a number of universal stages of cognitive development, Vygotsky believed that cognitive development varied across cultures.

Concept note-2: -Piaget proposed many applicable educational strategies, such as discovery learning with an emphasis on activity and play. However, Vygotsky incorporated the importance of social interactions and a co-constructed knowledge base to the theory of cognitive development.

Concept note-3: -Vygotsky differed from Piaget in that: Piaget believed that environmental stimuli contributed to development. Vygotsky’s theory suggests that learning precedes development.

Concept note-4: -Vygotsky, therefore, believed that inside the Zone of Proximal Development, learning can precede development, which means, that a child is able to learn skills that go beyond their natural maturity.

Concept note-5: -Thus, it is concluded that Piaget’s cognitive development theory advocates that cognitive development precedes symbolic language development in a child.
