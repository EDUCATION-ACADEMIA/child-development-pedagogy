What is the full name of REPA?

(A) Right to Education Protection Act

(B) ** Right to Education Protection Authority

(C) Right to Education Protest Act

(D) Right to Education Protest Authority

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The National Commission for Protection of Child Rights (NCPCR) has been designated as the agency to monitor provisions of the Right to Free and Compulsory Education (RTE) Act.

Concept note-2: -The law came into effect in the whole of India except the state of Jammu and Kashmir from 1 April 2010, the first time in the history of India a law was brought into force by a speech by the then Prime Minister Manmohan Singh.

Concept note-3: -It was passed by the Parliament in August 2009. When the Act came into force in 2010, India became one among 135 countries where education is a fundamental right of every child.

Concept note-4: -India became one of 135 countries to make education a fundamental right of every child when the Act came into force on 1 April 2010. The Act makes education a fundamental right of every child between the ages of 6 and 14 and specifies minimum norms in elementary schools.
