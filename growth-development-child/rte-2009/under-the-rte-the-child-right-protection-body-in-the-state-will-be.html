Under the RTE, the child right protection body in the State will be

(A) SIERT

(B) ** SCPCR

(C) SDMC

(D) None of the above

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The National Commission for Protection of Child Rights (NCPCR) has been designated as the agency to monitor provisions of the Right to Free and Compulsory Education (RTE) Act.

Concept note-2: -NCPCR Services Inquiring into complaints relating to violations/deprivation of ‘children’s right to education under RTE Act, 2009; Monitoring the implementation of the Protection of Children from Sexual Offences(POCSO), Act, 2012; Redressing Public Grievances pertaining to NCPCR.

Concept note-3: -Under this, the commission’s role is to ensure that all programs, policies, laws, and administration frameworks function according to the principles of children’s rights as per the Indian Constitution and the UN Convention on the Rights of the Child.

Concept note-4: -Thus, it is concluded that Section 24 of RTE deals with the duties of teachers. Section 11: Appropriate Government to provide for pre-school education. Section 7: Sharing of financial and other responsibilities.
