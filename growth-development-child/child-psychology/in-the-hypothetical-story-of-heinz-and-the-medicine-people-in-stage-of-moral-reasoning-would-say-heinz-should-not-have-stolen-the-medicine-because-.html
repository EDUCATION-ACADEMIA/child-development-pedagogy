In the hypothetical story of Heinz and the medicine, people in stage of moral reasoning would say Heinz should NOT have stolen the medicine because ____

(A) ** It is illegal to steal

(B) He might get caught

(C) His wife will judge him

(D) The druggist wouldn’t have stolen from him if their roles were reversed

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Stage one (obedience): Heinz should not steal the medicine because he will consequently be put in prison which will mean he is a bad person.

Concept note-2: -Stage five (human rights): Heinz should steal the medicine because everyone has a right to choose life, regardless of the law. Or: Heinz should not steal the medicine because the scientist has a right to fair compensation. Even if his wife is sick it does not make his actions right.

Concept note-3: -Heinz can steal the drug, but should be punished by the law. This decision helps Heinz save his wife, but Heinz will be kept in prison. Though Heinz took a moral decision, he had to undergo the punishment. This is a Conventional level of moral thinking.

Concept note-4: -Stage 4 (law-and-order): Heinz should not steal the medicine because the law prohibits stealing, making it illegal. Or: Heinz should steal the drug for his wife but also take the prescribed punishment for the crime as well as paying the druggist what he is owed.

Concept note-5: -Law and Order Orientation YES: As her husband, Heinz has a duty to save his wife’s life so he should steal the drug. But it’s wrong to steal, so Heinz should be prepared to accept the penalty for breaking the law.
