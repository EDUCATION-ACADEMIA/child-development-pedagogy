When a 10 month old finds a hidden toy this demonstrates ____, to understand objects still exist even when out of sight.

(A) Core knowledge

(B) Video deficit

(C) ** Object permanence

(D) Executive function

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Your baby is learning that people and objects exist even when they can’t see or hear them. Object permanence is one of the development milestones that your infant will learn during their first year of life.

Concept note-2: -Your baby will begin to understand the concept of object permanence when she is around 7 or 8 months old. You can tell that your baby is starting to understand object permanence if she starts looking around for a toy you’ve just hidden. Separation anxiety usually starts around this time, too.

Concept note-3: -In a nutshell, object permanence means your baby understands that things they can’t see-you, their cup, a pet-still exist. If you hide a favorite toy when playing with a very young baby, what happens? They might seem briefly confused or upset but then quickly give up on looking for it.

Concept note-4: -Object permanence describes a child’s ability to know that objects continue to exist even though they can no longer be seen or heard. If you have ever played a game of “peek-a-boo” with a very young child, then you probably understand how this works.
