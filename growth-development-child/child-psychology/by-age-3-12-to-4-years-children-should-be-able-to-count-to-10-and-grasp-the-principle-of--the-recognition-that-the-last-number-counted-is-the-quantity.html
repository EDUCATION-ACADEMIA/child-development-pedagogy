By age 3 1/2 to 4 years children should be able to count to 10 and grasp the principle of ____, the recognition that the last number counted is the quantity

(A) Reversibility

(B) Phonology

(C) ** Cardinality

(D) Ordinality

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The average 4-year-old can count up to ten, although he may not get the numbers in the right order every time. One big hang-up in going higher? Those pesky numbers like 11 and 20.

Concept note-2: -The average child can count up to “ten” at 4 years of age, however it is normal for children to still be learning to count to 5 while others are able to correctly count to forty.

Concept note-3: -Between 3 and 4 years of age, they’ll also be more adept at counting small sets of objects-“two oranges, four straws” and so on. Most children aren’t able to identify numerals or write them, though, until they’re 4 or 5.
