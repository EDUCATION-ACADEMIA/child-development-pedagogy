A way of thinking about your own intelligence and abilities; it is the unchanging way you view your morals, ideals, and thoughts.

(A) ** Fixed Mindset

(B) Growth Mindset

(C) Metacognition

(D) Insight

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -A fixed-mindset essentially means that you believe your attributes and abilities are inherently fixed and unchanging. Most of how you think about yourself in regard to personality, drive, setbacks, and goals grows directly out of your mindset.

Concept note-2: -Intelligence mindset refers to one’s belief that either intelligence is a malleable trait that can improve with effort-a “growth” mindset-or is a relatively stable trait-a “fixed” mindset.

Concept note-3: -What is a Fixed Mindset. A fixed mindset describes children (and adults) who believe their intelligence, talents and personalities are fixed traits that cannot grow. They believe we are born with a certain level of ability (or special skills) and we are unable to improve our level abilities over time.

Concept note-4: -A fixed mindset is the belief that your intelligence, talents and other abilities are set in stone. You believe that you’re born with a particular set of skills and that you can’t change them. If you have a fixed mindset, you will likely fear that you may not be smart or talented enough to achieve your goals .

Concept note-5: -A growth mindset simply means that people believe their intelligence and talents can be improved through effort and actions. A growth mindset also recognizes that setbacks are a necessary part of the learning process and allows people to ‘bounce back’ by increasing motivational effort.
