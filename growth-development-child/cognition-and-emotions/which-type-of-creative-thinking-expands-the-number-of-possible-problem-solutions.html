Which type of creative thinking expands the number of possible problem solutions?

(A) ** Divergent Thinking

(B) Convergent Thinking

(C) Intrinsic Motivation

(D) Availability Heuristic

(E) Representative Heuristic

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Divergent thinking is relevant here because it reflects the ability to generate many solutions to a problem or prompt (i.e., fluency).

Concept note-2: -In summary, creativity is the ability to come up with new, original, unique solutions to problems or ideas. Creativity makes use of divergent thinking, which is solving problems with many possible solutions, as opposed to convergent thinking, which is solving problems with a single, correct answer.

Concept note-3: -Divergent thinking is a type of creative process where several solutions and ideas are offered to address a challenge. This way of thinking can help increase creativity and innovation in problem-solving.

Concept note-4: -Convergent thinking focuses on finding one well-defined solution to a problem. Divergent thinking is the opposite of convergent thinking and involves more creativity. In this piece, we’ll explain the differences between convergent and divergent thinking in the problem-solving process.

Concept note-5: -Divergent thinking is a thought process or method used to generate creative ideas by exploring many possible solutions. It typically occurs in a spontaneous, free-flowing, “non-linear” manner, such that many ideas are generated in an emergent cognitive fashion.
