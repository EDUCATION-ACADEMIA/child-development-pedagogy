COGNITION AND EMOTIONS

[SOURCES]
One day, a girl with whom you’ve been trying to go out with socially gives you her phone number on a napkin. You put the napkin in your pocket, planning to save the number in your phone later. Unfortunately, you forget to save the number and wash your pants without taking out the napkin. Now, all you can piece together of the phone number are the first six digits plus “1", then dial the first six digits plus “2", etc. until you dial the correct number, you are solving the problem by ____

(A) using heuristics

(B) rote

(C) ** trial and error

(D) insight

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -To elaborate, a cultural barrier to problem-solving (including creative problem-solving) is related to socio-cultural values, norms, customs, and rituals. For example, when a person who believes in young earth fails to pass a geology class, a cultural barrier has occurred.

Concept note-2: -Illumination stage: Sometimes called the insight stage, illumination is when the “aha” moment happens. The light bulb clicks on as spontaneous new connections are formed and all of that material you’ve gathered comes together to present the solution to your problem.

Concept note-3: -Solution(By Examveda Team) High innovation would not be considered a barrier to entry. Innovation is about being creative and original in your work and thinking.
