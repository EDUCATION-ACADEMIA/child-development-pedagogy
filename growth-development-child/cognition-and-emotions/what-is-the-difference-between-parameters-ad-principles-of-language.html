What is the difference between parameters ad principles of language?

(A) Parameters are the parts of language in development which are common to all natural (human) languages. Principles are the variants between languages.

(B) ** Principles are the parts of language in development which are common to all natural (human) languages. Parameters are the variants between languages.

(C) Principles are the parts of L2 in development which are common to all natural (human) languages. Parameters are the variants between additional languages.

(D) Parameters are the parts of L2 in development which are common to all natural (human) languages. Principles are the variants between additional languages.

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Whereas principles restrict the number of possible natural grammars that may be acquired by a child, parameters allow to account for variation between languages. An example of a fundamental principle is the Extended Projection Principle (EPP) which requires every sentence to have a subject.

Concept note-2: -Principles and parameters is a framework within generative linguistics in which the syntax of a natural language is described in accordance with general principles (i.e. abstract rules or grammars) and specific parameters (i.e. markers, switches) that for particular languages are either turned on or off.

Concept note-3: -Parameters are properties that individual languages have that differentiate them from other languages. It has been assumed that there is a universal set of parameters for languages to draw upon, though there is no hard core evidence to support this interesting view. However, we shall assume it here without argument.

Concept note-4: -Language learning (LL) is the process of actively trying to learn and understand a language. LL happens more consciously and is more likely the result of formal teaching. Perhaps, like how a second language is learned. On the other hand, language acquisition is the process of gaining language knowledge naturally.
