The Magical number 7 plus or minus 2 is associated with what type of memory?

(A) ** SHORT TERM MEMORY

(B) LONG TERM MEMORY

(C) SENSORY MEMORY

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The Magic number 7 (plus or minus two) provides evidence for the capacity of short term memory. Most adults can store between 5 and 9 items in their short-term memory. This idea was put forward by Miller (1956) and he called it the magic number 7.

Concept note-2: -The Magical Number Seven experiment purports that the number of objects an average human can hold in working memory is 7 ± 2. What this means is that the human memory capacity typically includes strings of words or concepts ranging from 5–9.

Concept note-3: -The magical number 4 in short-term memory: A reconsideration of mental storage capacity.

Concept note-4: -Short-term memory refers to the information processed by the individual in a short period of time. Working memory performs this processing. Long-term memory allows us to store information for long periods of time. This information may be retrieved consciously (explicit memory) or unconsciously (implicit memory).
