EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -An emotion-arousing stimulus has two simultaneous effects, causing bodily arousal via the sympathetic nervous system and a subjective experience via the cortex.

Concept note-2: -Cannon-Bard Theory: theory that an emotion arousing stimulus simultaneously triggers physiological responses AND the subjective experience of emotion.

Concept note-3: -According to the Cannon-Bard theory of emotion, the experience of an emotion is accompanied by physiological arousal. Thus, according to this model of emotion, as we become aware of danger, our heart rate also increases.

Concept note-4: -The Cannon-Bard theory of emotion suggests that physical and emotional reactions to stimuli are experienced independently and at the same time. Research into emotional processes in the brain is ongoing, and theories continue to evolve.

Concept note-5: -According to Cannon-Bard theory of emotion, physiological arousal and emotional experience occur simultaneously, yet independently. This theory was proposed in the 1920s and early 1930s by Walter B. Cannon and Philip Bard.
