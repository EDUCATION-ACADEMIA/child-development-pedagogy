Negative attitude held by a person about the members of a particular social group.

(A) ** Prejudice

(B) Discrimination

(C) Ethnocentrism

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Prejudice is described as “an unfair negative attitude toward a social group or a member of that group” (Dovidio and Gaertner 1999, p. 101). In the field of social psychology, prejudice and the concepts of stereotypes and discrimination are among the core aspects in the area of group perception.

Concept note-2: -Prejudice is a negative attitude and feeling toward an individual based solely on one’s membership in a particular social group (Allport, 1954; Brown, 2010). Prejudice is common against people who are members of an unfamiliar cultural group.

Concept note-3: -Prejudice is defined as negative attitudes toward the members of specific social groups.

Concept note-4: -prejudice. negative attitude held by a person about the members of a particular social group. discrimination. treating people differently because of prejudice toward the social group to which they belong.

Concept note-5: -It relates to a way of looking at facts, and in this sense all beliefs and attitudes partake of prejudice to some extent. Prejudice may also refer only to those beliefs and attitudes which place the objects of the attitudes at a clear advantage or disadvantage.
