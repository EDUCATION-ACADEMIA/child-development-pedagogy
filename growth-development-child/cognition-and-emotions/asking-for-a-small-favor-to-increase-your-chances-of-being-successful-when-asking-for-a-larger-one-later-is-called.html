Asking for a small favor to increase your chances of being successful when asking for a larger one later is called:

(A) reciprocity norm

(B) ** foot-in-the-door phenomenon.

(C) low ball technique

(D) door-in-the-face phenomenon

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The foot-in-the-door technique is when a small request is initially made in order to get a person to later agree to a bigger request. An example of this is when a friend asks to borrow a small amount of money, then later asks to borrow a larger amount.

Concept note-2: -Foot-in-the-door (FITD) technique is a compliance tactic that aims at getting a person to agree to a large request by having him or her agree to a modest request first.

Concept note-3: -The foot-in-the-door phenomenon is the tendency for people who have first agreed to a small request to comply later with a larger request.

Concept note-4: -The opposite of the foot-in-the-door technique, door-in-the-face starts out with a large request that you know the prospect will decline followed immediately by a smaller request (the second request being what you really wanted the prospect to do).
