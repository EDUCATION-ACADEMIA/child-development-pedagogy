How many phonemes are in the word odd?

(A) 1

(B) ** 2

(C) 3

(D) 4

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Despite there being just 26 letters in the English language there are approximately 44 unique sounds, also known as phonemes. The 44 sounds help distinguish one word or meaning from another.

Concept note-2: -They will then start to put these sounds together, to make short words, such as: cat, nap, pin, tap, etc. This is called blending sounds. They will learn that each of these words have three distinct sounds (phonemes). For example, cat has the three sounds: /c/ /a/ and /t/.

Concept note-3: -The symbols: k, c, and q are three different graphemes used to represent one phoneme. The words: keep, come, and quit use three different graphemes to represent one phoneme: /k/. A grapheme my consist of more than one letter, representing the phoneme. A letter and a grapheme are not the same thing.
