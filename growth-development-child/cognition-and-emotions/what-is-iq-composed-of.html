What is IQ composed of?

(A) mental age

(B) GPA

(C) chronological age

(D) ** mental & chronological age

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Historically, IQ was a score obtained by dividing a person’s mental age score, obtained by administering an intelligence test, by the person’s chronological age, both expressed in terms of years and months. The resulting fraction (quotient) was multiplied by 100 to obtain the IQ score.

Concept note-2: -There are different kinds of IQ tests, but most analyze your visual, mathematical and language abilities as well as your memory and information processing speed. A licensed psychologist administers a series of subtests; the results are then combined into one score: your IQ.

Concept note-3: -What is IQ? IQ, (from “intelligence quotient”), a number used to express the relative intelligence of a person. It is one of many intelligence tests.

Concept note-4: -The four areas identified are verbal comprehension, perceptual reasoning, working memory and processing speed.

Concept note-5: -The IQ score can be calculated with the formula of IQ = (intelligence age/actual age) x100. Here is a short list of the most common IQ tests (intelligence quotient test), other than MentalUP’s online IQ test that you or your children may want to take: Stanford-Binet (SB-V) Wechsler Adult Intelligence Scale (WAIS-IV)
