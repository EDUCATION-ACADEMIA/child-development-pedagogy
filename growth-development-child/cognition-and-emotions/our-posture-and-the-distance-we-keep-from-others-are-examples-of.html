Our posture and the distance we keep from others are examples of

(A) reciprocity

(B) social perceptions

(C) ** nonverbal communication

(D) emotional appeals

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The way you move and carry yourself communicates a wealth of information to the world. This type of nonverbal communication includes your posture, bearing, stance, and the subtle movements you make.

Concept note-2: -Posture, facial expressions, and eye contact are examples of nonverbal messages. We all use these cues in daily conversation, even involuntarily. Nonverbal communication also involves the way we present ourselves to others.

Concept note-3: -In humans, posture can provide a significant amount of important information through nonverbal communication. Psychological studies have also demonstrated the effects of body posture on emotions. This research can be traced back to Charles Darwinâ€™s studies of emotion and movement in humans and animals.

Concept note-4: -Posture and movement can tell you how someone is feeling. For example, an arm-crossed posture sends the message that you are not open to new ideas or solutions. Or, the leg-crossed posture is known as a posture of defiance and defensiveness. Posture and movement send information about attitude.
