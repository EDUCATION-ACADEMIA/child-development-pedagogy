What are the three stages of memory in order?

(A) Sensory memory, long-term memory, short-term memory

(B) ** Sensory memory, short-term memory, long-term memory

(C) Short-term memory, long-term memory, sensory memory

(D) Long-term memory, short-term memory, sensory memory

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -According to this approach (see Figure 8.4 “Memory Duration”), information begins in sensory memory, moves to short-term memory, and eventually moves to long-term memory. But not all information makes it through all three stages; most of it is forgotten.

Concept note-2: -Stages of Memory Creation The brain has three types of memory processes: sensory register, short-term memory, and long-term memory.

Concept note-3: -Overview – Three Stages of Memory There are three memory stages: sensory, short-term, and long-term. Information processing begins in sensory memory, moves to short-term memory, and eventually moves into long-term memory. Information that you come across on a daily basis may move through the three stages of memory.

Concept note-4: -Our discussion will focus on the three processes that are central to long-term memory: encoding, storage, and retrieval.

Concept note-5: -Stage 1: Encoding Encoding occurs when we pay attention to information. For example, if you are trying to remember a list of groceries, you will need to pay attention to the items on the list in order to encode them into your memory. Information is encoded into a format that can be stored in our memory.
