Explanation of other people’s behavior based on personality or external factors

(A) ** attribution theory

(B) scapegoat

(C) self-serving bias

(D) matching hypothesis

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -External. Attribution theory proposes that the attributions people make about events and behavior can be classed as either internal or external. In an internal, or dispositional, attribution, people infer that an event or a person’s behavior is due to personal factors such as traits, abilities, or feelings.

Concept note-2: -Attribution theory assumes that people try to determine why people do what they do, i.e., attribute causes to behavior. A person seeking to understand why another person did something may attribute one or more causes to that behavior.

Concept note-3: -External attributions are explanations that stress environmental or situational factors, such as task difficulty, social influences, and the physical characteristics of a particular environment (Ross 1977). Fundamental to attribution theory is this “internal-external or disposition-situation dichotomy” (Ross 1977, p.

Concept note-4: -Attribution is a term used in psychology which deals with how individuals perceive the causes of everyday experience, as being either external or internal. Models to explain this process are called attribution theory.

Concept note-5: -Explanation: Dispositional attribution is when we attribute a person’s actions to their internal dispositions, such as personality, talents, emotional state, etc. We see the behavior as being caused by something internal to that person as opposed to attributing it to their environment.
