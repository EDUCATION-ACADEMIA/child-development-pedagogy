This nativist theorist proposed that humans are equipped with an innate language acquisition device.

(A) Gerd Gigerenzer

(B) Daniel Kahneman

(C) Steven Pinker

(D) ** Noam Chomsky

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The Nativist Perspective According to Chomsky’s theory, infants have an innate ability to learn language. From a very early age, we’re able to understand the basics of language. For instance, Chomsky argued, children are able to understand the appropriate order of words from a young age.

Concept note-2: -In the mid-twentieth century, Noam Chomsky developed language acquisition theory. This theory asserts that every child is born with a language acquisition device (LAD). The LAD is a hypothetical tool that helps children quickly learn and understand language.

Concept note-3: -Noam Chomsky was a pioneer of the Language Acquisition Device theory, in which he states that humans have an innate ability to learn language.
