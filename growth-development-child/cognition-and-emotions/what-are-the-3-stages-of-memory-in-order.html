What are the 3 stages of memory in order?

(A) Long-term, short-term, sensory

(B) short-term, long-term, sensory

(C) ** sensory, short-term, long-term

(D) sensory, long-term, short-term

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -According to this approach (see Figure 9.4, “Memory Duration”), information begins in sensory memory, moves to short-term memory, and eventually moves to long-term memory. But not all information makes it through all three stages; most of it is forgotten.

Concept note-2: -Stages of Memory Creation The brain has three types of memory processes: sensory register, short-term memory, and long-term memory.

Concept note-3: -Overview – Three Stages of Memory There are three memory stages: sensory, short-term, and long-term. Information processing begins in sensory memory, moves to short-term memory, and eventually moves into long-term memory. Information that you come across on a daily basis may move through the three stages of memory.

Concept note-4: -Sensory memory is fleeting unless we pay attention to it. Short-term memory serves as a temporary holding tank for things that we do pay some attention to, but it has limited storage capacity.
