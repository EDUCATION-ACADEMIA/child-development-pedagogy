COGNITION AND EMOTIONS

[SOURCES]
A prototype is a:

(A) Step-by-step procedure for solving problems

(B) Mental grouping of similar objects, events or people

(C) ** Best example of a particular category

(D) Simple thinking strategy for solving problems efficiently

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -A prototype is a mental object that represents the average members of a particular category. It often has all of the categoryâ€™s core features. This particular mental object is usually used in the process of object categorization.

Concept note-2: -A prototype is the BEST example or cognitive representation of something within a certain category. Prototypes are used to enhance memory and recall, since you can keep a prototype of something and then match new, similar things to the prototype in order to identify, categorize, or store this new thing.

Concept note-3: -The prototype of a category can be understood in lay terms by the object or member of a class most often associated with that class. The prototype is the center of the class, with all other members moving progressively further from the prototype, which leads to the gradation of categories.

Concept note-4: -Another technique used by your brain to organize information is the identification of prototypes for the concepts you have developed. A prototype is the best example or representation of a concept.

Concept note-5: -A category a set of objects that can be treated as equivalent in some way. For example, consider the following categories: trucks, wireless devices, weddings, psychopaths, and trout. Although the objects in a given category are different from one another, they have many commonalities.
