J.P. Guilford structure of intellect model consists of three dimensions namely

(A) Numerical abilities, spatial relations and memory

(B) acquisition component, meta component and performance component

(C) Inductive reasoning, word fluency and verbal comprehension

(D) ** Operations, contents and products

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The Structure of Intellect was first introduced by Joy Paul Guilford [1], a noted psychologist and psychometrist. The Structure Of Intellect theory of human intelligence consists of three core components: operations, contents, and products, which are known as “dimensions".

Concept note-2: -SI theory comprises up to 180 different intellectual abilities organized along three dimensions: operations, content, and products.

Concept note-3: -Summary of the Model Guilford’s Structure of Intellect organizes the various abilities associated with creativity and IQ into three abilities: content, product and operations.

Concept note-4: -six products: units, classes, relations, systems, transformations, implications. five contents: visual, auditory, symbolic, semantic, behavioral. Figure 9. Guilford’s structure of intellect model.
