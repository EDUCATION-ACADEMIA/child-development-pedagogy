What type of assessment method does Role-Play falls into?

(A) Oral Examination

(B) Body Languageg

(C) ** Practical Performance

(D) Written Examination

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Consider using role plays and simulations for both formative feedback and summative assessment, rather than introducing them only at the end of the course as a summmative assessment.

Concept note-2: -Role-playing is a form of assessment that requires students to take a role. This can include acting; as in skits, plays, or fake interviews, or taking a role as in an accountant, journalist; or world leader and performing the duties of that position for an amount of time.

Concept note-3: -Role plays can be part of Assessment Centres run by graduate employers. They give employers the opportunity to assess how you perform in an imaginary situation which is likely to mirror a scenario you may come across within the role.

Concept note-4: -Role-plays are a method of assessment in which a learner demonstrates skill competency within a realistic simulation.
