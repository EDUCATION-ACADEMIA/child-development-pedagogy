ADHD

[SOURCES]
What to do if you think you have ADHD

(A) Establish structure and stick to it.

(B) Encourage exercise and sleep.

(C) Set clear expectations.

(D) Seek outside help for better information and treatment.

(E) ** all of the above

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Adults who think they may have ADHD should talk to their health care provider. Primary care providers routinely diagnose and treat ADHD and may refer individuals to mental health professionals. If you need help starting the conversation, check out NIMH’s Tips for Talking With Your Health Care Provider fact sheet.

Concept note-2: -If you think you or your child may have attention deficit hyperactivity disorder (ADHD), speak to a GP. If you’re worried about your child, it may help to speak to their teachers, before seeing a GP, to find out if they have any concerns about your child’s behaviour.

Concept note-3: -If you are concerned about whether a child might have ADHD, the first step is to talk with a healthcare provider to find out if the symptoms fit the diagnosis. The diagnosis can be made by a mental health professional, like a psychologist or psychiatrist, or by a primary care provider, like a pediatrician.
