In 2030, approximately how many people will have diabetes?

(A) 117 million

(B) ** 170 million

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The total number of people living with diabetes is projected to rise to 643 million by 2030 and 783 million by 2045. 3 in 4 adults with diabetes live in low-and middle-income countries. Almost 1 in 2 (240 million) adults living with diabetes are undiagnosed. Diabetes caused 6.7 million deaths.

Concept note-2: -Without urgent and sufficient actions, it is predicted that 578 million people will have diabetes in 2030 and the number will increase by 51% (700 million) in 2045.

Concept note-3: -More than 37 million Americans have diabetes (about 1 in 10), and approximately 90-95% of them have type 2 diabetes. Type 2 diabetes most often develops in people over age 45, but more and more children, teens, and young adults are also developing it.

Concept note-4: -The global diabetes prevalence in 2019 is estimated to be 9.3% (463 million people), rising to 10.2% (578 million) by 2030 and 10.9% (700 million) by 2045.
