Which of the following best defines operant conditioning?

(A) occurs when an individualâ€™s old response becomes attached to a new stimulus

(B) ** learning that occurs as a result of the consequences of behavior

(C) the gradual disappearance of a conditioned response

(D) studied salivation responses in dogs

(E) conditioned an 11-month-old boy to fear laboratory rats

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The answer is B. Organisms learn from the consequences of their behavior. In operant conditioning, a behavior is either reinforced or encouraged through a series of punishments and reinforcements.

Concept note-2: -Operant conditioning occurs when a consequence (positive or negative reinforcement or punishment) is paired or ceases to be paired with a behavior and it increases or decreases the likelihood that behavior will recur.

Concept note-3: -Operant conditioning, also called instrumental conditioning, is a learning process where behaviors are modified through the association of stimuli with reinforcement or punishment.

Concept note-4: -Operant conditioning, on the other hand, is learning that occurs based on the consequences of behaviour and can involve the learning of new actions.

Concept note-5: -The basic concept behind operant conditioning is that a stimulus (antecedent) leads to a behavior, which then leads to a consequence. This form of conditioning involves reinforcers, both positive and negative, as well as primary, secondary, and generalized. Primary reinforcers are things like food, shelter, and water.
