What are the processes of observational learning?

(A) ** Attention, Retention, Motor production, Motivation

(B) Forming, Storming, Norming & Performing

(C) Input, Decision-Making & Output

(D) Intra-Psychological Learning & Inter-Psychological Learning

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -It refers to the process of performing or reproducing the learned action. It refers to the process of motivating learners to repeat the observational learning. Hence, it could be concluded that Attention → Retention → Reproduction → Motivation is the correct sequence of four elements of observational learning.

Concept note-2: -Observational learning is a major component of Bandura’s social learning theory. He also emphasized that four conditions were necessary in any form of observing and modeling behavior: attention, retention, reproduction, and motivation.

Concept note-3: -– Albert Bandura As the creator of the concept of social learning theory, Bandura proposes five essential steps in order for the learning to take place: observation, attention, retention, reproduction, and motivation.

Concept note-4: -According to Bandura social learning involves attention, retention, motor reproduction and motivation. Since learners have to pay attention to the model, remember the behaviour being observed, be able to replicate the action they observed, and be motivated to demonstrate what they have learned.

Concept note-5: -Bandura’s theory of social learning Bandura proposed that this type of learning involved four different stages – attention, retention, reproduction and motivation.
