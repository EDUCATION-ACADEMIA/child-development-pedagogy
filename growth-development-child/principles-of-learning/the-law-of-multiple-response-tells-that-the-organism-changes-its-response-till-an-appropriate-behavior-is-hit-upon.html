PRINCIPLES OF LEARNING

[SOURCES]
The Law of Multiple Response tells that the organism changes its response till an appropriate behavior is hit upon

(A) ** TRUE

(B) FALSE

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -According to it the organism varies or changes its responses till an appropriate behaviour is hit upon. Without varying the responses, the correct response for the solution might never be elicited.

Concept note-2: -Thorndike’s Law of Multiple Responses states that when one response does not bring satisfaction, it will initiate a new response. For this reason, the animal goes on producing responses until some response brings satisfaction.

Concept note-3: -Thorndike. The law of effect stated that those behavioral responses that were most closely followed by a satisfying result were most likely to become established patterns and to occur again in response to the same stimulus.

Concept note-4: -This work led to Thorndike’s Laws. According to these Laws, learning is achieved when an individual is able to form associations between a particular stimulus and a response. The three main laws are the Law of Readiness, the Law of Exercise, and the Law of Effect.

Concept note-5: -The law of associative shifting describes a process whereby a response may shift from one stimulus to another. Learning consists of the formation of connections between stimuli and responses, and such connections are created whenever a response is followed by a satisfier/reward.
