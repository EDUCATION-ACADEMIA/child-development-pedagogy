What are some factors that make L2 learners and bilinguals different from monolinguals?

(A) Innate talent for language acquisition

(B) ** Brain plasticity

(C) ** Critical period (age)

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Motivation, attitude, age, intelligence, aptitude, cognitive style, and personality are considered as factors that greatly influence someone in the process of his or her second language acquisition.

Concept note-2: -Bilingual children show some advantages in socio-cognitive development when compared to monolinguals, particularly in understanding the beliefs of others, picking out the important variables to solve a problem, and entertaining two possible interpretations of the same stimulus at once.

Concept note-3: -Results revealed a critical period ending around 17 years of age for the most effective acquisition of a second language (L2). The findings of a late cutoff indicate the age range of late childhood to late adolescence as crucial for learning an L2.
