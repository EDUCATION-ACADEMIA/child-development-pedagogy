[SOURCES]
Investigators detected a tendency for those who excelled in one of Thurstone’s seven primary mental abilities to also demonstrate high levels of competence in other abilities. This provided some evidence of

(A) predictive validity.

(B) ** general intelligence.

(C) emotional intelligence.

(D) neural plasticity.

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Psychologist Louis L. Thurstone (1887–1955) focused on seven primary mental abilities rather than a single, general ability.

Concept note-2: -Spearman believed that General Intelligence was represented as an intelligence factor underlying specific mental abilities, which he referred to as “g-factor”. He explained that this g factor was responsible for a person’s overall performance on mental ability tests.

Concept note-3: -The seven primary mental abilities in Thurstone’s model were verbal comprehension, word fluency, number facility, spatial visualization, associative memory, perceptual speed and reasoning.

Concept note-4: -An aptitude test is designed to assess what a person is capable of doing or to predict what a person is able to learn or do given the right education and instruction.
