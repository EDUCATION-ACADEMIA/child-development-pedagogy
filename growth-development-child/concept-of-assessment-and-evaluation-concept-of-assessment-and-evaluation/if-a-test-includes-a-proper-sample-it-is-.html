CONCEPT OF ASSESSMENT AND EVALUATION CONCEPT OF ASSESSMENT AND EVALUATION

[SOURCES]
If a test includes a proper SAMPLE, it is ____

(A) ** content valid.

(B) face valid.

(C) construct valid

(D) criterion valid.

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Content validity evaluates how well an instrument (like a test) covers all relevant parts of the construct it aims to measure. Here, a construct is a theoretical concept, theme, or idea: in particular, one that cannot usually be measured directly. Content validity is one of the four types of measurement validity.

Concept note-2: -Content validity is the degree to which a test or assessment instrument evaluates all aspects of the topic, construct, or behavior that it is designed to measure. Do the items fully cover the subject? High content validity indicates that the test fully covers the topic for the target audience.

Concept note-3: -Validity is commonly understood as referring to the outcomes of an assessment and whether the evidence known about the assessment supports the way in which the results are used. Test users need to be sure that the particular assessment they are using is appropriate for the purpose they have identified.

Concept note-4: -For example, suppose a professor wants to test the overall knowledge of his students in the subject of elementary statistics. His test would have content validity if: The test covers every topic of elementary statistics that he taught in the class.

Concept note-5: -Content validity assesses whether a test is representative of all aspects of the construct. To produce valid results, the content of a test, survey or measurement method must cover all relevant parts of the subject it aims to measure.
