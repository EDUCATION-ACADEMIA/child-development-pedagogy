True or False-All behavioral adaptations serve a purpose to help the animal survive.

(A) ** True

(B) False

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Adaptations: any physical or behavioral characteristics of an organism that help it to survive in its environment. Behavioral adaptation: something an animal does usually in response to some type of external stimulus in order to survive. Hibernating during winter is an example of a behavioral adaptation.

Concept note-2: -There are three types of adaptations: structural, physiological, and behavioral. Structural adaptations are how the animalâ€™s body functions or looks on the outside. Body parts (like feet and ears) and body coverings (like fur and scales) are structural adaptations.

Concept note-3: -Adaptations are any behavioral or physical characteristics of an organism that helps it survive in its environment. Structural adaptations are inherited and develop in a population over a long period of time. If an animal changes the way it acts to stay alive, this is called a behavioral adaptation.

Concept note-4: -What are behavioural adaptations? Generally speaking, behavioural adaptation refers to a behaviour that an animal has developed or changed over time in order to increase its chances of survival. As a result, their species can populate, reproduce and grow.
