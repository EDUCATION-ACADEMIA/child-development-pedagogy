GROWTH AND DEVELOPMENT

[SOURCES]
By the age of 2 years child’s vocabulary is

(A) ** 250 words

(B) 500 words

(C) 1000 words

(D) 10, 000 words

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Between the ages of 2 and 3, most children: Speak in two-and three-word phrases or sentences. Use at least 200 words and as many as 1, 000 words.

Concept note-2: -At age 2, most kids say at least 2 words together. By 30 months, they are saying 50 words or more and are understood about half of the time. They are using words like “I, ” “me, ” or “we.” By 30 months, most kids can follow 2-step instructions, like “Pick up the ball and bring it to Daddy."

Concept note-3: -Children begin to expand their vocabulary rapidly between the ages of 3 and 5. During this time, children should be able to speak around 250 to 500 words. Preschoolers should also be able to answer simple questions, state their name and age, tell stories, and speak in sentences of five to six words by the age of 5.

Concept note-4: -"They should really be learning a new word a day or two or three words a day, ” around this time, Boerner says. Learning at that rate should give your toddler between 900 and 1000 words by the time they turn 3.
