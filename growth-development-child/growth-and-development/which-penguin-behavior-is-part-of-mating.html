Which penguin behavior is part of mating?

(A) Flying to a warmer climate in order to lay eggs

(B) Building a large nest to accommodate the eggs

(C) The female catching large amounts of fish

(D) ** The male bringing small stones to the female

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Adélie, gentoo, and chinstrap penguins are known to take stones from other nests. A penguin returning to the nest sometimes brings its mate a stone as a courtship gesture. Chinstrap penguins usually construct nests with perimeters of eight to ten stones, just enough to prevent eggs from rolling away.

Concept note-2: -A Pebble Proposal During courtship, a male penguin will find the smoothest pebble to give to a female as a gift. If she likes the offering, she’ll place it in the nest and the two will continue building up their little pebble mound in preparation for the eggs.

Concept note-3: -Males and females recognise each other by their calls and it is remarkable how quickly a female can locate her mate among thousands of penguins when she comes home.

Concept note-4: -During courtship, males serenade prospective mates by throwing their heads back, pointing their beaks to the sky, and emitting a series of hoarse trills and squawks.

Concept note-5: -BREEDING: Emperor penguins begin courtship in March or April and are serially monogamous, typically taking one mate per year. The female lays one egg in May or June, transfers the egg to the male, and returns to sea to feed while the male incubates the egg in his brood pouch for about 65 days.
