The type of development that deals with relationships and interactions with others.

(A) ** Social

(B) Physical

(C) Mental

(D) Emotional

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Social development refers to the long-term changes in relationships and interactions involving self, peers, and family. It includes both positive changes, such as how friendships develop, and negative changes, such as aggression or bullying.

Concept note-2: -Social interactions are how we act and react to those around us; they form the basis of any relationship between two or more people, whether youâ€™re aged two or 102. These interactions include physical movements, body language, talking and conversations, playing, sharing, and more.

Concept note-3: -Social interaction is the process of reciprocal influence exercised by individuals over one another during social encounters. Usually it refers to face-to-face encounters in which people are physically present with one another for a specified duration.

Concept note-4: -The third stage of psychosocial development takes place during the preschool years. At this point in psychosocial development, children begin to assert their power and control over the world through directing play and other social interactions.

Concept note-5: -Society passes through well-defined stages in the course of its development. They are nomadic hunting and gathering, rural agrarian, urban, commercial, industrial, and post-industrial societies.
