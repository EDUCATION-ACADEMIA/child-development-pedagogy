A child who is 13-18 years old

(A) School age

(B) Preschooler

(C) ** Adolescent

(D) Someone too old to be called a child

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The teen years are also called adolescence. This is a time for growth spurts and puberty changes (sexual maturation). A teen may grow several inches in several months, followed by a time of very slow growth. Then they may have another growth spurt.

Concept note-2: -The teenage years are also called adolescence. Adolescence is a time for growth spurts and puberty changes. An adolescent may grow several inches in several months followed by a period of very slow growth, then have another growth spurt.

Concept note-3: -Early adolescence (ages 10 to 13).

Concept note-4: -13 is in the early half of adolescents, teen years overall. 13 is a minor in law and are definitely still kids since being young but they are past the actual child age and into the next phase in life. Child comes from childhood referring to the time between birth and puberty and people in the age group is just that.

Concept note-5: -There are three stages of adolescence, which include early adolescence (10 to 13 years), middle adolescence (14 to 17 years), and late adolescence/young adulthood (18 to 21 years and beyond).
