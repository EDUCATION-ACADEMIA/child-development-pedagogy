When an amoeba goes through the cell cycle and divides, it has ____ ?

(A) grown

(B) repaired

(C) ** reproduced

(D) photosynthesized

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Amoeba reproduces asexually through binary fission. In this process, an individual divides itself into two daughter cells.

Concept note-2: -Amoeba reproduces asexually by the process called binary fission. Binary fission occurs in single-celled organisms belonging to Kingdom Monera, and Protista (Amoeba and paramecium). In this process, the parent organism divides into two halves, each half forming an independent daughter organism.

Concept note-3: -An Amoeba reproduces by the process of binary fission. In this process, the nucleus firstly divides to form two equal parts. This is followed by the division of cytoplasm.

Concept note-4: -Among lower eukaryotes such as Acanthamoeba, the mode of reproduction is generally considered to be asexual, i.e., mother cell divides into two daughter cells via binary fission or in simple terms, amoebae do not need any genetic material from another organism to reproduce.

Concept note-5: -Amoeba multiplies by simple mitotic cell division giving rise to two daughter Amoebae. Here, the growth is synchronous with reproduction, i.e., increase in number.
