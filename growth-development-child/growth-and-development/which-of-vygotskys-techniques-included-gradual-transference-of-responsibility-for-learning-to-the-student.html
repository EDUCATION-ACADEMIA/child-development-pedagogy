Which of Vygotsky’s techniques included gradual transference of responsibility for learning to the student?

(A) reciprocal teaching

(B) zone of proximal development

(C) ** scaffolding

(D) peer collaboration

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Vygotsky’s scaffolding is a theory that focuses on a student’s ability to learn information through the help of a more informed individual. When used effectively, scaffolding can help a student learn content they wouldn’t have been able to process on their own.

Concept note-2: -Instructional scaffolding is a process through which a teacher adds supports for students in order to enhance learning and aid in the mastery of tasks. The teacher does this by systematically building on students’ experiences and knowledge as they are learning new skills.

Concept note-3: -For example, when infants are learning how to walk, they often start by holding onto the clothes or hands of an adult or older child, who guides them. The infant will continue to do this until they have enough skills and strength to walk on their own.
