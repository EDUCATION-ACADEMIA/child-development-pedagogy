How did the Spanish influence the Texas cowboys?

(A) religion, economy and beans

(B) petrobussines and production of computers

(C) ** culture, language and cattle ranches

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Spanish ranching as it was practiced in Texas formed the basis for the American cattle industry, which drew many of its original cattle from the mission herds. The Spanish also brought to the San Antonio valley a specialized method of farming that used irrigation.

Concept note-2: -The stateâ€™s name is derived from the word Tejas, a Spanish rendering of the Caddo word for friends or allies. Many place names such as San Antonio, Corpus Christi, and El Paso, are of Spanish origin. The culture still thrives in Texas via food, entertainment, language, and celebrations such as Cinco de Mayo.

Concept note-3: -Spanish culture in what became the state of Texas would be based primarily on-and filtered through-Mexican culture, a very distinct and dynamic cultural tradition that derived only partly from Spanish culture. Many of those descended from Spaniards were mestizo or mixed blood.

Concept note-4: -The origins of the vaquero tradition come from Spain, beginning with the hacienda system of medieval Spain. This style of cattle ranching spread throughout much of the Iberian peninsula, and it was later imported to the Americas.
