Which stage of the human life cycle follows the foetus?

(A) Childhood.

(B) ** Baby.

(C) Old age.

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The six stages of human development include the foetus, infancy, toddler years, childhood, puberty, adolescence, adulthood, middle age and senior years.

Concept note-2: -After the embryonic stage, the fetal stage begins and your baby is called a fetus. This stage runs from the 11th week until birth. Your baby will grow longer and gain weight quicker. His or her organs and body parts will continue to develop.

Concept note-3: -During fertilization, the sperm and egg unite in one of the fallopian tubes to form a zygote. Then the zygote travels down the fallopian tube, where it becomes a morula. Once it reaches the uterus, the morula becomes a blastocyst. The blastocyst then burrows into the uterine lining-a process called implantation.

Concept note-4: -The development of a newborn during pregnancy is an amazing process. There are 4 stages of the pregnancy timeline including: 1st trimester, 2nd trimester, 3rdtrimester & birth.

Concept note-5: -This begins the final stage of development, called the fetal stage. By the end of this month, the fetus is fully formed, with all organs and extremities in place that will continue to grow and develop throughout the pregnancy.
