What is the scientific name for the shoot that first emerges from a seed during germination?

(A) Testa

(B) Radicle

(C) Cotyledon

(D) ** Plumule

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The radicle is the first part of a seedling (a growing plant embryo) to emerge from the seed during the process of germination.

Concept note-2: -The radicle (primary embryonic root) emerges from the seed first to enhance water uptake; it is protected by a root cap produced by the root apical meristem.

Concept note-3: -Embryo of a seed has two parts, namely plumule and radicle. Plumule gives rise to shoot and Radicle gives rise to root of plant.

Concept note-4: -Introduction. Germination is the fundamental process in which the sown plant seeds are grown into young plants or seedlings. Based on their growing conditions and the fate of the cotyledons, the process of germination is classified into two main types: Epigeal Germination. Hypogeal Germination.

Concept note-5: -Plumules: It is part of the seed embryo, which develops into the shoot after seed germination. Also called a baby plant or new plant arising from the seed embryo.
