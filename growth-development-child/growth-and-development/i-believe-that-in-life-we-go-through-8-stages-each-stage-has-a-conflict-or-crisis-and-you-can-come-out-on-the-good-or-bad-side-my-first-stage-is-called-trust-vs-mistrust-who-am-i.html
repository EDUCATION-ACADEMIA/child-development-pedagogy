GROWTH AND DEVELOPMENT

[SOURCES]
I believe that in life, we go through 8 stages. Each stage has a conflict or crisis and you can come out on the good or bad side. My first stage is called trust vs. mistrust. Who am I?

(A) Jean Piaget

(B) Lev Vygotsky

(C) ** Erik Erikson

(D) Howard Gardner

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Understanding Erikson’s 8 Stages of Development Toddler – Autonomy versus shame and doubt. Preschool-age – Initiative versus guilt. School-age – Industry versus inferiority. Adolescence – Identity versus identity confusion.

Concept note-2: -Erikson maintained that personality develops in a predetermined order through eight stages of psychosocial development, from infancy to adulthood. During each stage, the person experiences a psychosocial crisis which could have a positive or negative outcome for personality development.

Concept note-3: -Erikson suggested that at each stage there is a major conflict that will ultimately contribute to a person’s mental health and wellbeing later in life.

Concept note-4: -Stage 1: Trust vs. mistrust By providing these basic needs, you teach them that they can depend on you. This builds within them the psychological strength of trust. Feeling secure and safe, your infant will be ready to experience the world.
