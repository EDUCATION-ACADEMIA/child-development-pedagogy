Amniotic eggs are eggs kept inside a parent’s body.

(A) True

(B) ** False

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -What type of animals provide parental care for its young? MAMMALS provide parental care to their young until they are independent. Amniotic eggs are eggs kept inside a parents body.

Concept note-2: -The offspring of some animals develop inside an egg laid outside of the parent’s body. Most animals without backbones lay eggs. The eggs of land vertebrates such as reptiles and birds, that develop in an egg outside of the parent’s body are called amniotic eggs.

Concept note-3: -The development of the amniotic egg was an important adaptation, because it allowed amniotes to reproduce on land.

Concept note-4: -The placenta is a temporary organ that forms in your uterus during pregnancy. It attaches to your uterine wall and provides nutrients and oxygen to your baby through the umbilical cord. Certain conditions of the placenta can cause pregnancy complications.

Concept note-5: -Amphibian Eggs Unlike other tetrapod vertebrates (reptiles, birds, and mammals), amphibians do not produce amniotic eggs.
