This theorist studied the emotional development of man. His 8 theories include:Trust vs. Mistrust, and Autonomy vs. Doubt.

(A) ** Erik Erikson

(B) Arnold Gesell

(C) Jean Piaget

(D) Abraham Maslow

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Erik Erikson’s (1958, 1963) psychosocial development theory proposes that our personality develops through eight stages, from infancy to old age.

Concept note-2: -Erikson’s theory postulates that people advance through the stages of development based on how they adjust to social crises throughout their lives. These social crises instruct how individuals react to the surrounding world.

Concept note-3: -the first of Erikson’s eight stages of psychosocial development, between birth and 18 months of age. During this stage, the infant either comes to view other people and himself or herself as trustworthy or comes to develop a fundamental distrust of his or her environment.

Concept note-4: -Understanding Erikson’s 8 Stages of Development Infancy – Basic trust versus mistrust. Toddler – Autonomy versus shame and doubt. Preschool-age – Initiative versus guilt. School-age – Industry versus inferiority.
