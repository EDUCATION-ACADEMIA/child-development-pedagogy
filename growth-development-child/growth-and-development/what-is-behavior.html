What is Behavior?

(A) ** response to a stimulus

(B) learning

(C) response to food

(D) having fun

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -In behavioral psychology (i.e., classical and operant conditioning), a stimulus constitutes the basis for behavior. The stimulus–response model emphasizes the relation between stimulus and behavior rather than an animal’s internal processes (i.e., in the nervous system).

Concept note-2: -Examples of stimuli and their responses: You are hungry so you eat some food. A rabbit gets scared so it runs away. You are cold so you put on a jacket.

Concept note-3: -or US behavioral response. noun. a change in behaviour in response to a stimulus.

Concept note-4: -Final answer: The reaction to a stimulus is called response.

Concept note-5: -Example of a stimulus and a response: If you accidentally touch a hot object, you automatically withdraw your hand. The heat of the hot object is the stimulus and you, withdrawing your hand is the response to the stimulus.
