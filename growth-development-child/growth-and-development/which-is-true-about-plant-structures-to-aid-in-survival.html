GROWTH AND DEVELOPMENT

[SOURCES]
Which is true about plant structures to aid in survival

(A) Plants are tall

(B) ** plants have bright colors to attract pollinators

(C) Plants have no special structures

(D) Plants have green leaves to attract mates.

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -They are the main structure for reproduction, so they aid in pollination to continue the development of new plants. Flower petals attract pollinators who then fertilize the plants, seeds are developed, and some plants produce fruits and vegetables, and pollinators are required for this process.

Concept note-2: -Petals. The colorful, thin structures that surround the sexual parts of the flower. Not only attract pollinators, but also protect the pistil and stamen. May also produce a scent.

Concept note-3: -The petals of a flower give it its unique shape, color, and smell. It’s their job to attract pollinators, like insects and hummingbirds, to the flower.

Concept note-4: -Plants produce nectar to attract pollinators. As the pollinator moves from flower to flower collecting nectar, they are also moving pollen from flower to flower. Certain fruits and seeds will not be produced if their flowers are not pollinated.

Concept note-5: -Brightly colored flowers attract pollinators, and some flowers have ultraviolet pigments that only insects such as bees can see. Studies have shown that flowers that are red or have stripes attract bees most. The stripes act as a “landing strip” that indicates where the nectar and pollen are found in the flower.
