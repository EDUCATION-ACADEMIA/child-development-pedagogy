A force that influences your behavior is called ____

(A) theory

(B) ** motivation

(C) competence

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Motivation is the reason for which humans and other animals initiate, continue, or terminate a behavior at a given time. Motivational states are commonly understood as forces acting within the agent that create a disposition to engage in goal-directed behavior.

Concept note-2: -Motivation is defined as a “psychological process responsible for initiating, maintaining, supporting or stopping a behaviour. It is in a way the force that drives us to act and think in one way or another” (Universalis).

Concept note-3: -Motivation is an important factor in everyday life. Our basic behaviors and feelings are affected by our inner drive to succeed over life’s challenges while we set goals for ourselves. Our motivation also promotes our feelings of competence and self-worth as we achieve our goals.

Concept note-4: -Motivation is the word derived from the word ‘motive’ which means needs, desires, wants or drives within the individuals. It is the process of stimulating people to actions to accomplish the goals. In the work goal context the psychological factors stimulating the people’s behaviour can be-desire for money. success.

Concept note-5: -Motivated behaviors are fundamental components of vertebrate life. These behaviors control an animal’s interactions with those goal objects in the environment that are important for the survival of the individual and the species; for example, food, water, or conspecifics.
