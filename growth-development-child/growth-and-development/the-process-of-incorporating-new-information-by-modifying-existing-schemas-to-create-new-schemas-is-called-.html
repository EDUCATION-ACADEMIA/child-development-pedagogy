The process of incorporating new information by modifying existing schemas to create new schemas is called ____

(A) Assimilation

(B) ** Accommodation

(C) Equilibrium

(D) Disequilibrium

(E) Schema

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Accommodation: Involves altering existing ideas or schemas as a result of new experiences. The process of accommodation involves altering oneâ€™s existing schemas, or ideas, as a result of new information or new experiences.

Concept note-2: -Accommodation involves altering existing schemas, or ideas, as a result of new information or new experiences. New schemas may also be developed during this process.

Concept note-3: -Assimilation is the process of integrating new knowledge into existing schemas by editing the new knowledge to ensure an acceptable fit. In other words, assimilation involves updating schemas without changing the structure of those schemas.

Concept note-4: -Assimilation describes how we interpret new experiences in terms of our current understanding, so in terms of our current schemas. Accommodation describes how we later adjust our schemas to better incorporate new experiences.

Concept note-5: -The processes through which schemas are adjusted or changed are known as assimilation and accommodation. In assimilation, new information is incorporated into pre-existing schemas.
