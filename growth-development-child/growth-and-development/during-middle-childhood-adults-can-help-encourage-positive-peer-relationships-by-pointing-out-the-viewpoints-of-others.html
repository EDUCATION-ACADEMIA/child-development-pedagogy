GROWTH DEVELOPMENT CHILD

GROWTH AND DEVELOPMENT

[SOURCES]
During middle childhood, adults can help encourage positive peer relationships by pointing out the viewpoints of others.

(A) ** True

(B) False

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -During middle childhood, adults can help encourage positive peer relationships by pointing out the viewpoints of others. During adolescence, physical growth is faster than any time other than infancy. The pituitary gland signals the endocrine system to release hormones, which is the onset of puberty.

Concept note-2: -Emotional/Social Changes Children in this age group might: Start to form stronger, more complex friendships and peer relationships. It becomes more emotionally important to have friends, especially of the same sex. Experience more peer pressure.

Concept note-3: -Self-Concept Children in middle childhood have a more realistic sense of self than do those in early childhood. That exaggerated sense of self as “biggest” or “smartest” or “tallest” gives way to an understanding of one’s strengths and weaknesses.

Concept note-4: -Many girls and boys experience a prepubescent growth spurt, but this growth spurt tends to happen earlier in girls (around age 9-10) than it does in boys (around age 11-12). Because of this, girls are often taller than boys at the end of middle childhood.
