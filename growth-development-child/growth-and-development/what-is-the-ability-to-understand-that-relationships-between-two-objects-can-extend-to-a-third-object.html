What is the ability to understand that relationships between two objects can extend to a third object?

(A) ** transitivity

(B) classification

(C) seriation

(D) conservation

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Transitive inference: It is the process of finding unknown relationships between objects by using multiple previous information.

Concept note-2: -The ability to infer the relationship between two objects by understanding each objectâ€™s relationship to a third is called seriation.

Concept note-3: -Seriation involves the ability to put things in order based on quantity or magnitude.

Concept note-4: -Seriation: the ability to sort objects in an order according to size, shape or any other characteristic.

Concept note-5: -Classification is the skill of sorting or grouping items by similar characteristics, such as colors, shapes or sizes. Children naturally classify toys by type and sort crayons by color.
