GROWTH AND DEVELOPMENT

[SOURCES]
What change is:At 6 months, most infants can sit and can reach for objects.

(A) ** Physical

(B) Emotional

(C) Interpersonal

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Around age 6 months, many babies begin sitting alone after being positioned upright. Improving hand-eye coordination. Your baby will probably grasp your finger, a rattle or a soft object. Anything within reach is likely to end up in your baby’s mouth.

Concept note-2: -Able to hold almost all weight when supported in a standing position. Able to transfer objects from one hand to the other. Able to lift chest and head while on stomach, holding the weight on hands (often occurs by 4 months) Able to pick up a dropped object.

Concept note-3: -Not only will your baby laugh and babble, but they also will likely be able to sit up without support and may show readiness for solid foods. Plus, they may even start sleeping longer stretches at night, which means you might finally get more shut-eye.

Concept note-4: -As infants and toddlers grow, their determination to master movement, balance, and fine-and gross-motor skills remains strong. Rolling and crawling occur as infants develop skill in using large-muscle groups. Grasping and picking up objects with fingers are part of small-muscle growth.

Concept note-5: -gross motor-your baby will move their head up while lying down on their tummy. fine motor-your baby’s tight fists will begin to loosen, their eyes may fix on objects and they will prefer to look at faces. communication and hearing-your baby will be alert to sounds and throaty noises.
