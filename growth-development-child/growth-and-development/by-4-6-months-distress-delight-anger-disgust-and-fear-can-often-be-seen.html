GROWTH DEVELOPMENT CHILD

GROWTH AND DEVELOPMENT

[SOURCES]
By 4-6 months distress, delight, anger, disgust, and fear can often be seen

(A) ** true

(B) false

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Emotional Development: Newborns show excitement. By 4 to 6 months of age, distress, delight, anger, disgust, and fear can often be seen. By 12 months of age, elation and affection for adults is evident.

Concept note-2: -Social and emotional milestones at 6 months Is usually happy and responds to the emotions of others. Starting to differentiate between familiar faces and strangers. Enjoys playing with you and others. Has fun looking at himself in a mirror.

Concept note-3: -For the first 6 months, babies express emotions based on how they feel at the moment. Around month 7, babies start to develop emotions like fear and anger. From 8-11 months, babies are more sensitive to approval/disapproval and separation anxiety is likely to peak.

Concept note-4: -Pleasure is expressed as laughter at 3 to 5 months of age, and displeasure becomes more specific as fear, sadness, or anger between ages 6 and 8 months.
