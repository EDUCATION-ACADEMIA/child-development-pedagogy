MASLOW HIERARCHY

[SOURCES]
Willingness to continue to grow and challenge yourself is an example of ____

(A) ** Self Actualization

(B) Safety

(C) Love and Belonging

(D) Self Esteem

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -(Maslow, 1943). Extrapolating from this quote, we can see self-actualization in examples like: An artist who has never made a profit on his art, but he still paints because it is fulfilling and makes him happy. A woman who finds joy in achieving mastery in a niche hobby.

Concept note-2: -Self-actualization is the final stage in the linear growth of an individual. Maslow believed that in order to achieve this state of personal fulfilment, the person must first satisfy the preceding needs (i.e. physiological, safety, love/belonging, and esteem, in that order).

Concept note-3: -self-actualization, in psychology, a concept regarding the process by which an individual reaches his or her full potential. It was originally introduced by Kurt Goldstein, a physician specializing in neuroanatomy and psychiatry in the early half of the 20th century.
