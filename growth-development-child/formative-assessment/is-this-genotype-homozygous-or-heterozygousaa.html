Is this genotype homozygous or heterozygous?aa

(A) ** homozygous

(B) heterozygous

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -If an organism has two copies of the same allele, for example AA or aa, it is homozygous for that trait. If the organism has one copy of two different alleles, for example Aa, it is heterozygous.

Concept note-2: -The term “homozygous” is used to describe the pairs “AA” and “aa” because the alleles in the pair are the same, i.e. both dominant or both recessive. In contrast, the term “heterozygous” is used to describe the allelic pair, “Aa”.

Concept note-3: -An organism with one dominant allele and one recessive allele is said to have a heterozygous genotype. In our example, this genotype is written Bb. Finally, the genotype of an organism with two recessive alleles is called homozygous recessive.

Concept note-4: -An individual who is homozygous dominant for a particular gene will have two dominant “A” alleles for that gene. An individual who is homozygous recessive will have the “aa” genotype, two recessive “a” alleles.

Concept note-5: -Answer and Explanation: In a cross Aa x Aa, this indicates that each parent is heterozygous. This means there is one dominant allele and one recessive.
