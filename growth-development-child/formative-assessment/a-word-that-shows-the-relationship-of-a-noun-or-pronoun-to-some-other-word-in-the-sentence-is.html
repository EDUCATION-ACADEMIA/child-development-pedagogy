A word that shows the relationship of a noun or pronoun to some other word in the sentence is

(A) Noun

(B) ** Preposition

(C) Verb

(D) Adverb

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -A preposition shows the relationship that exists between a noun or pronoun and some other word in the sentence. The preposition and the noun or pronoun become the prepositional phrase.

Concept note-2: -A preposition is a word that expresses relations. Paired with a noun, a preposition can tell you precisely where an object is or the means by which something is accomplished. Prepositions are easy to spot because they typically follow the noun or pronoun that they modify.

Concept note-3: -A noun identifies a person, animal or thing. Pronouns are words like he, she, yourself, mine, who, this and someone. Pronouns commonly refer to or fill the position of a noun or noun phrase. A determiner specifies the kind of reference a noun has. Common determiners are: the, those, my, her, both, all, several and no.

Concept note-4: -The noun or pronoun being related by the preposition is called the object of the preposition.

Concept note-5: -Simple Preposition These are among the most common type of prepositions. The prepositions used to express the relationship the Nouns and Pronouns of a sentence have with the rest of the words in it are called Simple Prepositions.
