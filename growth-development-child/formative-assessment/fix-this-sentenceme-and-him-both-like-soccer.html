FORMATIVE ASSESSMENT

[SOURCES]
Fix this sentence:“Me and him both like soccer.”

(A) Him and me both like soccer.

(B) Him and I both like soccer.

(C) ** He and I both like soccer.

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Both as a pronoun refers to two people or things and is always plural. Both is/are correct.

Concept note-2: -Nancy is the subject who is taking you to the park and applying an action on you. “I” is the subject of a sentence, while “me” is the object. Although this seems like such a simple rule, many people tend to overlook it. The corrected version of this sentence would be, “Nancy took Wesley and me to the amusement park.”

Concept note-3: -To recap, ask yourself if it’s a conjunction (followed by a subject and verb) or preposition (no verb; only a noun or noun phrase). If it’s a conjunction, use as. If it’s a preposition, ask yourself if the meaning is similar to or same as. If it’s similar to, use like.

Concept note-4: -Example sentences: I like pizza. Shelly likes dogs (she likes dogs). Tyson likes Madonna (he likes Madonna).
