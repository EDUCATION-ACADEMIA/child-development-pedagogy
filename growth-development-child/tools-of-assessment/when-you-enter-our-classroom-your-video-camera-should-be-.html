[SOURCES]
When you enter our classroom, your video camera should be ____

(A) turned off

(B) turned towards your pet

(C) ** on and focused on you

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Through turning your camera on, you connect with your peers and build personal bonds. This will make it much less awkward when you eventually return to school and have no idea which classmate is who. While this may be annoying at first, turning on your camera holds you accountable for your conduct in class.

Concept note-2: -Letting others see what you’re doing increases productivity. You are much more likely to pay attention and stay on task when the teacher and peers can see you in person, and it’s no different online. Turning on your camera allows others to see when you are doing, and it is likely you would want to look productive.

Concept note-3: -Students experience greater levels of trust and rapport because of verbal and nonverbal cues that occur when video cameras are turned on in video conferencing. The use of video cameras in synchronous distant learning creates intimacy and immediacy, leading to teacher–learner social presence, which leads to dialog.

Concept note-4: -According to his survey, the three main reasons why students do not turn on their cameras during online class are privacy, self-conscious, and not camera-ready or their appearance.
