Which one of the following disorder is related to language comprehension?

(A) Apraxia

(B) Dyscalculia

(C) Dyslexia

(D) ** Aphasia

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Aphasia is a condition that has a connection or an overlap with several other speech-related disorders and problems, such as dysarthria, dysphasia and apraxia. Aphasia: This is the overall term for a brain-connected problem with language abilities, including speaking or understanding other people speaking.

Concept note-2: -Dysgraphia. Dysgraphia may refer to either difficulty with language or spelling-based aspects of written expression. Dysgraphia can occur alone or can co-occur with dyslexia and/or other learning disabilities. The cognitive–linguistic aspects of dysgraphia are involved in the writing process and the writing product.

Concept note-3: -Aphasia is the medical term for full loss of language, while dysphasia stands for partial loss of language. The word aphasia is now commonly used to describe both conditions.
