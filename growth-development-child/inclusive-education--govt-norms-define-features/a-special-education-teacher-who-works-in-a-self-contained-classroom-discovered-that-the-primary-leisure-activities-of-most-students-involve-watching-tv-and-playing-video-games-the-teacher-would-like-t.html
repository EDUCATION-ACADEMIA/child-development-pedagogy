A Special Education Teacher who works in a self-contained classroom discovered that the primary leisure activities of most students involve watching TV, and playing video games-the teacher would like to increase the student’s participation in different types of activities. Which of the following strategies would best meet her goals?

(A) Having students conduct a survey to determine the types of leisure activities within the same-age peers

(B) Inviting guest speakers from various school clubs and extracurricular activity groups to present information to students.

(C) Assigning students to research various types of leisure activities

(D) ** Integrating time into the weekly schedule consistently for students to participate in various types of leisure activities.

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -An inductive approach is when the rule is inferred through some form of guided discovery. (The teacher gives the students a means to discover the rule for themselves.)

Concept note-2: -The term “self-contained classroom” refers to a classroom, where a special education teacher is responsible for the instruction of all academic subjects. The classroom is typically separated from general education classrooms but within a neighborhood school.

Concept note-3: -Active learning approaches place a greater degree of responsibility on the learner than passive approaches such as lectures, but instructor guidance is still crucial in the active learning classroom.

Concept note-4: -A self-contained classroom is one in which the same group of students are taught multiple subjects by one educator throughout the day. This setting is usually found at the elementary level, but can be utilized in other grade levels.
