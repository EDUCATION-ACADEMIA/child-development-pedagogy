Social Stories™ can use a number of different sentence types. Which sentences below are types of sentences that might be included in a Social Story?A. Directive sentencesB. Cooperative sentencesC. Perspective sentencesD. Descriptive sentencesE. Affirmative sentences(AFIRM, 2015)

(A) A, B, C, D

(B) A, C, D

(C) B, D, E

(D) ** A, B, C, D, E

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -There are four basic sentence types used in Social Stories: descriptive, perspective, affirmative, and directive. Each has a specific role. Less frequently used are partial sentences. Descriptive Sentences: truthful, opinion and assumption free.

Concept note-2: -Directive sentences: Identify possible responses (e.g., “If I need a break, I can tell the teacher or put the break card on my desk and go to the beanbag.”) • Gently direct behavior (e.g., I will try to .”).

Concept note-3: -A Social Story/Article has a title and introduction that clearly identifies the topic, a body that adds detail, and a conclusion that reinforces and summarizes the information.
