Which of the following is an appropriate example of a Fixed Ratio schedule of reinforcement

(A) Random guided reading checks

(B) Fishing

(C) Receiving your paycheck every other week

(D) ** Sales Commission

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -For example, a fixed-ratio schedule might involve the delivery of a reward for every fifth response. After the subject responds to the stimulus five times, a reward is delivered.

Concept note-2: -Fixed-Ratio Schedules This schedule produces a high, steady rate of responding with only a brief pause after the delivery of the reinforcer. An example of a fixed-ratio schedule would be delivering a food pellet to a rat after it presses a bar five times.

Concept note-3: -The fixed ratio schedule involves using a constant number of responses. For example, if the rabbit is reinforced every time it pulls the lever exactly five times, it would be reinforced on an FR 5 schedule. Ratio schedules involve reinforcement after an average number of responses have occurred.

Concept note-4: -Fixed ratio is a schedule of reinforcement. In this schedule, reinforcement is delivered after the completion of a number of responses. The required number of responses remains constant. The schedule is denoted as FR-#, with the number specifying the number of responses that must be produced to attain reinforcement.

Concept note-5: -e.g. Continuous schedules of reinforcement are often used in animal training. The trainer rewards the dog to teach it new tricks. When the dog does a new trick correctly, its behavior is reinforced every time by a treat (positive reinforcement).
