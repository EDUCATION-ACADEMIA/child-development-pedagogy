Any event or situation that evokes a response is known as?

(A) Acquistion

(B) ** Stimulus

(C) Reinforcement

(D) Shaping

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -stimulus any event or situation that evokes a response. respondent behavior behavior that occurs as an automatic response to some stimulus. In classical conditioning, we learn to associate two stimuli and thus to anticipate events. (A stimulus is any event or situation that evokes a response.)

Concept note-2: -Stimulus Â  any event or situation that evokes a response.

Concept note-3: -The first stimulus was one that evoked the response to be tested, without prior experience. These types of stimulus-response pairs are known as innate reflexes, and the stimulus is named an unconditioned stimulus (US) and its response an unconditioned response (UCR).

Concept note-4: -A conditioned stimulus is a neutral cue or event that produces an involuntary response after repeatedly being paired with an unconditioned stimulus that naturally elicits that behavior.

Concept note-5: -An unconditioned response is the naturally occurring response; an unconditioned stimulus is the stimulus that evokes an innate response.
