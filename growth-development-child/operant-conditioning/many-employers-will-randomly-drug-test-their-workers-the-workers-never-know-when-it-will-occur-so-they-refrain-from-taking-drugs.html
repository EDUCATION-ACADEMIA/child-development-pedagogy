Many employers will randomly drug test their workers. The workers never know when it will occur so they refrain from taking drugs.

(A) Fixed ratio

(B) Variable ratio

(C) Fixed interval

(D) ** Variable interval

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -a. Type of schedule: The type of reinforcement schedule represented by random drug testing is the variable-interval type of reinforcement schedule. In this type of schedule the time between reinforcements varies around some average rather than being fixed.

Concept note-2: -Tests are commonly used for five categories of drugs: Amphetamines; Cocaine Marijuana; Opiates; and Phencyclidine (PCP). Additional categories may include barbiturates, Benzodiazepines, ethanol (alcohol), hydrocodone, MDMA, methadone, methaqualone, or propoxyphene.

Concept note-3: -Random drug testing is unannounced and unexpected. Employees don’t know they’re testing until it’s time to test. An employer, or Third Party Administrator (TPA) like Lobdock Impairment Detection, uses a random selection process to select a percentage of employees in the drug testing program.

Concept note-4: -Urinalysis – A urine test is the most common form of pre-employment drug testing and is typically conducted once a conditional offer of employment has been sent. A urine test can show traces of drug use even after the effects of the drug have worn off and remain in the body for an extended period of time.
