OPERANT CONDITIONING

[SOURCES]
a problem-solving method in which multiple attempts are made to reach a solution. If the behavior “works” the behavior is repeated, if it doesn’t a new behavior is tried until success / solution is achieved.

(A) ** Trial and Error learning

(B) Avoidance Learning

(C) Operant learning

(D) Classical Learning

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Trial and error is a fundamental method of Problem solving. It is characterised by repeated, varied attempts which are continued until success, or until the agent stops trying. Until getting the right answer the problem is repeating through various steps. This type of problem solving is called trial and error method.

Concept note-2: -When using trial and error, you would continue to try different solutions until you solved your problem. Although trial and error is not typically one of the most time-efficient strategies, it is a commonly used one.

Concept note-3: -A heuristic is another type of problem solving strategy. While an algorithm must be followed exactly to produce a correct result, a heuristic is a general problem-solving framework (Tversky & Kahneman, 1974). You can think of these as mental shortcuts that are used to solve problems.

Concept note-4: -A trial-and-error approach to problem-solving involves trying a number of potential solutions to a particular issue, then ruling out those that do not work. If you’re not sure whether to buy a shirt in blue or green, for instance, you may try on each before deciding which one to purchase.
