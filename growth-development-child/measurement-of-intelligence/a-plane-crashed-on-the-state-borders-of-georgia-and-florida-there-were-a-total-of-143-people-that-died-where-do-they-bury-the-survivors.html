A plane crashed on the state borders of Georgia and Florida. There were a total of 143 people that died. Where do they bury the survivors?

(A) Georgia

(B) Florida

(C) ** They won’t let them rot.

(D) I don’t know

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -How was it possible that every single person in an airplane crash died, but two people survived? Because they were married.

Concept note-2: -Question:-“A plane crashed and every single person died but there were survivors. How is that possible?” This is an old puzzle; married survivors didn’t die. Explanation: The question specifies single (unmarried) people died, but that still leaves married people as possible survivors.

Concept note-3: -The National Transportation Safety Board reported that 95% of airplane occupants survived accidents, and about 40% of fatalities could have been prevented had passengers taken proper action. For some, however, surviving the impact is just half the battle.

Concept note-4: -1908–First Fatality in a Powered Aircraft On Sept. 17, 1908, a modified Wright Brothers aircraft crashed during a demonstration at Fort Myer, Va., seriously injuring pilot Orville Wright and killing the observer, U.S. Army Lt. Thomas E. Selfridge.
