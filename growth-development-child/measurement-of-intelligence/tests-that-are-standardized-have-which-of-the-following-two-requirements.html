Tests that are standardized have which of the following two requirements?

(A) test takers pass the test and it is graded equally

(B) ** test takers are given the same questions and it is graded in a standard way

(C) test takers are asked about different standards in multiple subjects

(D) test takers have high standards and they know it

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Once students are in high school and considering attending college or university, they will have to determine if they will have to take the SAT or ACT, which are standardized tests that are used to determine students academic readiness for higher education.

Concept note-2: -A standardized test is a test that is administered and scored in a consistent, or “standard", manner. Standardized tests are designed in such a way that the questions and interpretations are consistent and are administered and scored in a predetermined, standard manner.

Concept note-3: -In addition to the familiar multiple-choice format, standardized tests can include true-false questions, short-answer questions, essay questions, or a mix of question types.

Concept note-4: -Validity − The test has to be devised to measure what it claims to measure in order to be held as valid and usable. Reliability − This refers to the consistency of scores obtainedby an individual on the same test on two different occasions.
