Research on the language capabilities of apes clearly demonstrates that they have the capacity to

(A) vocalize the most common vowel sounds.

(B) acquire language vocabulary as rapidly as most children.

(C) ** communicate through the use of symbols.

(D) do all of these things.

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The correct answer is b. have the capacity to communicate meaning through symbols or gestures. Apes, notably gorillas, bonobos, and chimpanzees, can use symbols or gestures. They can learn from humans, but lack the vocal equipment to pronounce many human sounds.

Concept note-2: -Thus, ape language research provided strong support for the gestural theory of language by demonstrating that apes could communicate symbolically with visual rather than auditory communication systems.

Concept note-3: -The brain areas responsible for word meaning and grammar must mature and connections need to develop and strengthen. Concept note-5: -Most primates spend their lives in complex, tightly woven societies and need to frequently communicate with each other. They communicate with smells, sounds, visual messages, and touching. Non-human primates emphasize the use of body language.
