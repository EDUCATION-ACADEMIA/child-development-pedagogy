Which type of learner learns from hearing?

(A) visual

(B) ** auditory

(C) kinesthetic

(D) all of these

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Auditory. If you are an auditory learner, you learn by hearing and listening. You understand and remember things you have heard. You store information by the way it sounds, and you have an easier time understanding spoken instructions than written ones.

Concept note-2: -Auditory learning style – this means you learn by hearing and listening. Acquire knowledge by reading aloud • Hum and/or talk to yourself • Make comments like: ➢ “I hear you clearly.” ➢ “I’m wanting you to listen.” ➢ “This sounds good.”

Concept note-3: -What is auditory learning? Auditory learning means that a student learns most effectively by listening. They would prefer listening to a lecture over reading a textbook, or hearing the instructions for a project instead of figuring it out hands-on.

Concept note-4: -Auditory-Sometimes referred to as “aural” learners, auditory learners prefer listening to information that is presented to them vocally.

Concept note-5: -Auditory learners – absorb information by sound. Kinaesthetic learners – absorb information by moving.
