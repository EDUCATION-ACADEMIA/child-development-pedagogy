GROWTH DEVELOPMENT CHILD

MEASUREMENT OF INTELLIGENCE

[SOURCES]
At this age a child normally has an average vocabulary of about 10, 00 words

(A) 7-8 years of age

(B) 3-4 years of age

(C) 10-12 years of age

(D) ** 5-6 years of age

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -After children begin understanding words in the first year of life, their receptive vocabulary size increases rapidly. At age one, children recognize about 50 words; by age three, they recognize about 1, 000 words; and by age five, they recognize at least 10, 000 words (Shipley & McAfee, 2015).

Concept note-2: -5 By the time a child reaches school age and heads to kindergarten, he/she will have between a 2, 100-and 2, 200-word vocabulary. 6 The 6-year-old child typically has a 2, 600 word expressive vocabulary (words he or she says), and a receptive vocabulary (words he or she understands) of 20, 000–24, 000 words.

Concept note-3: -Vocabulary – Your child should use single words by 15 to 16 months of age at the latest. They should have a 10-word vocabulary by 18 months of age. Following directions – They should be able to follow simple directions by the time they are 21 months old.

Concept note-4: -Most “typical” 5-year-olds have a vocabulary of about 10, 000 words. When children are in school, they learn vocabulary at a rapid rate each year (Merritt, 2016).
