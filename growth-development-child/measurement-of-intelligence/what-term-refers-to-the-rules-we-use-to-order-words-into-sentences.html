What term refers to the rules we use to order words into sentences

(A) ** Syntax

(B) Semantics

(C) Morpheme

(D) Grammer

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -In linguistics, “syntax” refers to the rules that govern the ways in which words combine to form phrases, clauses, and sentences. The term “syntax” comes from the Greek, meaning “arrange together.” The term is also used to mean the study of the syntactic properties of a language.

Concept note-2: -The standard order of words in an English sentence is subject + verb + object.

Concept note-3: -Syntax covers topics like word order and grammar rules, such as subject-verb agreement or the correct placement of direct and indirect objects. Syntax is essential to understanding constituency, the term for multiple words acting as a single unit.

Concept note-4: -Syntactic Rules These are: English usually has a Subject-Verb-Object word order, unlike some other languages. A sentence must have a subject and a verb, and it must express a complete thought. Generally, it is best to express separate ideas in different sentences.

Concept note-5: -The way words are arranged in a sentence is called ‘word order’. Word order is a part of syntax because it decides how to build different kinds of sentences.
