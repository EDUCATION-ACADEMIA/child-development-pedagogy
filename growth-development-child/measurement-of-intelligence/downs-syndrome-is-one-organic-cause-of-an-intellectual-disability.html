Down’s syndrome is one organic cause of an intellectual disability.

(A) ** True

(B) False

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Down’s syndrome is the commonest identifiable cause of intellectual disability, accounting for around 15-20% of the intellectually disabled population. It is believed that people with Down’s syndrome have always existed.

Concept note-2: -A. Down’s syndrome is one organic cause of an intellectual disability. To be labeled with an intellectual disability, one must score below a 70 on intelligence tests and have limitations in at least two adaptive skill areas.

Concept note-3: -Down syndrome is the most common genetic cause of intellectual disability, affecting approximately 1 in every 700 children.

Concept note-4: -Down syndrome is a chromosomal condition that is associated with intellectual disability, a characteristic facial appearance, and weak muscle tone (hypotonia) in infancy. All affected individuals experience cognitive delays, but the intellectual disability is usually mild to moderate.

Concept note-5: -Cognitive impairment, problems with thinking and learning, is common in people with Down syndrome and usually ranges from mild to moderate. Only rarely is Down syndrome associated with severe cognitive impairment. Other common cognitive and behavioral problems may include1, 2, 3, 4: Short attention span.
