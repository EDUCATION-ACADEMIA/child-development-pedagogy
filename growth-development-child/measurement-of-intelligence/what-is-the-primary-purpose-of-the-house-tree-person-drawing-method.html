What is the primary purpose of the House Tree Person Drawing Method?

(A) ** Determine the main personality traits

(B) Tests attachment issues of children to their parents

(C) Use to construct blueprints for commercial building

(D) Determine the personality styles for house selection in newly weds

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The primary purpose of the HTP is to measure aspects of a person’s personality through interpretation of drawings and responses to questions. It provides clinically useful information about a person’s psychological, emotional, and mental health status.

Concept note-2: -The house-tree-person test (HTP) is a projective personality test, a type of exam in which the test taker responds to or provides ambiguous, abstract, or unstructured stimuli (often in the form of pictures or drawings).

Concept note-3: -The Tree-Drawing Test (TDT, Koch’s Baum Test) is a projective psychological examination often used for assessing personality in the developmental age [1]. Its easiness of administration makes it a useful tool to express self-image and emotional states with relatively little resistance.

Concept note-4: -This projective technique involves asking the subject to draw first a house, then a tree, and finally a person. The drawings are subsequently evaluated and analyzed for dynamic information relative to personality variables and interactions of the subject with his environment. Inventory: 1 Manual and Interpretive Guide.

Concept note-5: -It is concluded that there is a positive correlation between S-HTP drawing test and SAS for anxiety state of cancer patients (p<0.01). S-HTP drawing test and SAS have interrater reliability and test-retest reliability. Our findings indicate that the S-HTP drawing test could help in screening anxiety in cancer patients.
