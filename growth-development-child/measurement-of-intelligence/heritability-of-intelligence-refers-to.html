MEASUREMENT OF INTELLIGENCE

[SOURCES]
Heritability of intelligence refers to

(A) ** the extent to which a person’s intelligence is caused by genetics

(B) the effect of adoption on the intelligence of adopted children

(C) the amount of group variation in intelligence that can be attributed to genetics

(D) the extent to which the quality of schools and other environmental factors determine intelligence

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Explanation: Heritability or genetic influence on intelligence variation among group members is estimated to be approximately 50 to 80 percent. Certain scientists have suggested that this influence of genes becomes stronger in adulthood then it was in childhood.

Concept note-2: -Heritability of intelligence refers to: the amount of group variation in intelligence that can be attributed to genetics. The most widely used modern intelligence test was developed by: David Wechsler.

Concept note-3: -Like most aspects of human behavior and cognition, intelligence is a complex trait that is influenced by both genetic and environmental factors.

Concept note-4: -Conclusions From Genetic Studies In conclusion, twin studies show that individual differences in human intelligence can largely (50%–80%) be explained by genetic influences making intelligence one of the most heritable traits.
