What condition is an inability to form new memories?

(A) ** Anterograde Amnesia

(B) Psychogenic Amnesia

(C) Retrograde Amnesia

(D) Source Amnesia

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Anterograde amnesia is a type of memory loss that occurs when you can’t form new memories. In the most extreme cases, this means you permanently lose the ability to learn or retain any new information. On its own, this type of memory loss is rare. Anterograde amnesia is often temporary.

Concept note-2: -Anterograde Amnesia: Describes amnesia where you can’t form new memories after the event that caused the amnesia. Anterograde amnesia is far more common than retrograde.

Concept note-3: -Anterograde amnesia (AA) refers to an impaired capacity for new learning. Retrograde amnesia (RA) refers to the loss of information that was acquired before the onset of amnesia.

Concept note-4: -Anterograde amnesia deals more specifically with remembering new information. You may already have difficulty with long-term memories at this point. Symptoms of anterograde amnesia primarily affect short-term memory processing. This can cause confusion and frustration.

Concept note-5: -There are multiple potential causes of anterograde amnesia, all of which involve some sort of trauma or stress to the brain. Factors that can increase the risk of developing anterograde amnesia include: Drug use: Short-term anterograde amnesia may result from the use of certain drugs.
