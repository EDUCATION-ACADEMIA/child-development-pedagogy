What do aptitude tests do?

(A) Help you learn a new skill.

(B) ** Measures the ability to acquire new information used to predict future performance (SAT).

(C) Measures your knowledge.

(D) Measures my height.

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -In general, aptitude tests measure one’s competence in logical reasoning, numerical skills, or verbal ability; competency can be evaluated through problem-solving tasks and testing one’s critical thinking across various contexts.

Concept note-2: -aptitude test, examination that attempts to determine and measure a person’s ability to acquire, through future training, some specific set of skills (intellectual, motor, and so on).

Concept note-3: -Use of Aptitude Tests for Learning and Development As cognitive tests measure if a person has strong analytical, reasoning, attention to detail, decision making and problem-solving skills, they can be used to decide if an employee will struggle to perform well at their job or not.

Concept note-4: -Aptitude tests are administered before training is given. Aptitude tests are evaluated in terms of predictive validity, how well they can predict a person’s success in a particular area with training.

Concept note-5: -Aptitude Test. Both achievement and aptitude tests measure the strengths and abilities of the test-taker. However, aptitude tests focus on the potential someone has to learn new things while achievement tests focus on what has already been learned.
