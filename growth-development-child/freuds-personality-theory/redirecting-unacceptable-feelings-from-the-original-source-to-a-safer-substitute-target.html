FREUDS PERSONALITY THEORY

[SOURCES]
Redirecting unacceptable feelings from the original source to a safer substitute target

(A) Repression

(B) regression

(C) ** displacement

(D) sublimation

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Displacement is a psychological defense mechanism in which a person redirects a negative emotion from its original source to a less threatening recipient. A classic example of the defense is displaced aggression.

Concept note-2: -What is Sublimation? Sublimation is a defense mechanism that redirects energy from an unacceptable impulse or emotion into a more socially acceptable one. It’s seen as a mature form of behavior and is used to cope with difficult situations.

Concept note-3: -Displacement Displacement is the redirection of an impulse (usually aggression) onto a powerless substitute target. The target can be a person or an object that can serve as a symbolic substitute.

Concept note-4: -Displacement is a defense mechanism in which a person redirects an emotional reaction from the rightful recipient onto another person or object. For example, if a manager screams at an employee, the employee doesn’t scream back-but he may yell at his spouse later that night.

Concept note-5: -Reaction Formation Why do people behave this way? According to Freud, they are using reaction formation as a defense mechanism to hide their true feelings by behaving in the exact opposite manner.
