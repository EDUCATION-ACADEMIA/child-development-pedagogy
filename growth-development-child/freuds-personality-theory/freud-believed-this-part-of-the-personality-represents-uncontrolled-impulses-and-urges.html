FREUDS PERSONALITY THEORY

[SOURCES]
Freud believed this part of the personality represents uncontrolled impulses and urges

(A) ** Id

(B) Ego

(C) Superego

(D) Collective Unconscious

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The id is the primitive and instinctive component of personality. The id is a part of the unconscious that contains all the urges and impulses, including what is called the libido, a kind of generalized sexual energy that is used for everything from survival instincts to appreciation of art.

Concept note-2: -The most primitive part of the human mind, the id is the source of our bodily needs, wants, desires, and impulses. Freud believed that the id acts according to the “pleasure principle” – the psychic force that motivates the tendency to seek immediate gratification of any impulse.

Concept note-3: -According to Freudian theory, the id is the component of personality that forms the basis of our most primitive impulses. The id is entirely unconscious, and it drives our most important motivations, including the sexual drive (libido) and the aggressive or destructive drive (Thanatos).

Concept note-4: -In his theory of psychoanalysis, Sigmund Freud suggested that human personality was made up of three key elements. He called these the id, the ego, and the superego. According to Freud, it is the interaction of these three parts of the personality that influence how people think and behave.

Concept note-5: -According to Sigmund Freud’s psychoanalytic theory of personality, the superego is the component of personality composed of the internalized ideals that we have acquired from our parents and society. The superego works to suppress the urges of the id and tries to make the ego behave morally, rather than realistically.
