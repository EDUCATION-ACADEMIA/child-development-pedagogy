FREUDS PERSONALITY THEORY

[SOURCES]
This part of the mind was considered as a dump box for urges, feelings and ideas that are tied to anxiety, conflict and pain.

(A) conscious mind

(B) subconscious mind

(C) pre-conscious mind

(D) ** unconscious mind

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: –The id is impulsive and responds directly to instincts. Structure of the Psyche/ Mind Unconscious Freud felt that this part of the mind was not directly accessible to awareness. In part, he saw it as a dump box for urges, feelings and ideas that are tied to anxiety, conflict and pain.

Concept note-2: -The parts of the brain that perform the functions that Freud called the “id” are located mainly in the ERTAS and limbic system, whereas the parts that perform the functions he attributed to “the repressed” (or the “system unconscious”) are located mainly in the basal ganglia and cerebellum.

Concept note-3: -The unconscious mind is a reservoir of feelings, thoughts, urges, and memories that are outside of our conscious awareness. The unconscious contains contents that are unacceptable or unpleasant, such as feelings of pain, anxiety, or conflict.

Concept note-4: -Repression is the unconscious blocking of unpleasant emotions, thoughts, memories or impulses from conscious awareness. It is considered a defense mechanism for the brain to avoid processing distressing thoughts and push them into inaccessible recesses of consciousness.

Concept note-5: -In Sigmund Freud’s psychoanalytic theory of personality, the unconscious mind is defined as a reservoir of feelings, thoughts, urges, and memories that outside of conscious awareness.
