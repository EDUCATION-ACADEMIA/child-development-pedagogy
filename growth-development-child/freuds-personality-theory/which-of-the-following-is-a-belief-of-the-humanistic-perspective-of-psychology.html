Which of the following is a belief of the Humanistic perspective of psychology

(A) People are attempting to satisfy ID desires

(B) ** People are good and have free will

(C) Traits define personality

(D) We strive towards archetypes manifested in the collective unconscious

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Humanistic psychology focuses on each individual’s potential and stresses the importance of growth and self-actualization. The fundamental belief of humanistic psychology is that people are innately good and that mental and social problems result from deviations from this natural tendency.

Concept note-2: -Humanistic psychologists believe that an individual’s behavior isconnected to his inner feelings and self-image. Unlike the behaviorists, humanistic psychologists believe that humans arenot solely the product of their environment.

Concept note-3: -Humanistic psychologists believe that humans have free will. Humans are able to make their own decisions and are not determined by biological or environmental factors.

Concept note-4: -The humanistic perspective focuses on the positive image of what it means to be human. Human nature is viewed as basically good, and humanistic theorists focus on methods that allow fulfillment of potential. Abraham Maslow proposed that an individual is motivated by a hierarchy of needs.

Concept note-5: -humanistic psychology, a movement in psychology supporting the belief that humans, as individuals, are unique beings and should be recognized and treated as such by psychologists and psychiatrists. The movement grew in opposition to the two mainstream 20th-century trends in psychology, behaviourism and psychoanalysis.
