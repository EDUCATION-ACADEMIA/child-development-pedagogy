FREUDS PERSONALITY THEORY

[SOURCES]
____ theorists have determined five major characteristics of personality.

(A) ** Trait

(B) Cognitive

(C) Humanistic

(D) Psychodynamic

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The traits that constitute the five-factor model are extraversion, neuroticism, openness to experience, agreeableness, and conscientiousness.

Concept note-2: -Originally developed in 1949, the big 5 personality traits is a theory established by D. W. Fiske and later expanded upon by other researchers including Norman (1967), Smith (1967), Goldberg (1981), and McCrae & Costa (1987).

Concept note-3: -These five primary personality traits are extraversion (also often spelled extroversion), agreeableness, openness, conscientiousness, and neuroticism.

Concept note-4: -The Psychoanalytic Theory of Personality The psychoanalytic theory was developed by Sigmund Freud and argued that human personality comprises three elements, the id, the ego, and the superego. These three elements are said to work together to produce our character.

Concept note-5: -Psychoanalytic, humanistic, trait perspective and behaviorist theory are the four main personality theories.
