FREUDS PERSONALITY THEORY

[SOURCES]
According to Freud, the ____ contains instinctual and biological urges.

(A) ** id

(B) ego

(C) supergo

(D) ultraego

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -According to Freud’s psychoanalytic theory, the id is the primitive and instinctual part of the mind that contains sexual and aggressive drives and hidden memories, the super-ego operates as a moral conscience, and the ego is the realistic part that mediates between the desires of the id and the super-ego.

Concept note-2: -The Id. According to Freud, the id is the source of all psychic energy, making it the primary component of personality. 1. The id is the only component of personality that is present from birth. This aspect of personality is entirely unconscious and includes instinctive and primitive behaviors.

Concept note-3: -The id is the primitive and instinctive component of personality. It consists of all the inherited (i.e., biological) components of personality present at birth, including the sex (life) instinct – Eros (which contains the libido), and the aggressive (death) instinct – Thanatos.

Concept note-4: -In his theory of psychoanalysis, Sigmund Freud suggested that human personality was made up of three key elements. He called these the id, the ego, and the superego. According to Freud, it is the interaction of these three parts of the personality that influence how people think and behave.
