FREUDS PERSONALITY THEORY

[SOURCES]
Terms that begin with “self", like self-concept and self-efficacy are terms rooted in this perspective

(A) Trait Theory

(B) Psychodynamic Theory

(C) Social-Cognitive Theory

(D) Psychoanalytical Theory

(E) ** Humanistic Theory

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The self is the humanistic term for who we really are as a person. The self is our inner personality, and can be likened to the soul, or Freud’s psyche. The self is influenced by the experiences a person has in their life, and out interpretations of those experiences.

Concept note-2: -People pay attention to themselves, noticing both their internal states and responses and their external behavior. Through such self-awareness, people collect information about themselves. Self-concept is built from this information and continues to develop as people expand their ideas about who they are.

Concept note-3: -Introduction. The humanistic perspective is a way of evaluating an individual as a whole, rather than looking at them only through a smaller aspect of their person. It is a branch of psychology that relates to the idea of being entirely unique and your own individual.

Concept note-4: -Second, we distinguish the four main conceptual units that constitute the various selves of self-presentation. These are the public self, the self-concept, the actual or behavioral self, and the ideal self.
