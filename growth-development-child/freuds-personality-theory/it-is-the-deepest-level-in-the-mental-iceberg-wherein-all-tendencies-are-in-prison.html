[SOURCES]
It is the deepest level in the mental iceberg wherein all tendencies are in prison.

(A) EGO IDEAL

(B) ** UNCONSCIOUS

(C) SUPEREGO

(D) SUBCONSCIOUS

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -It is the deepest level in the mental iceberg wherein all tendencies are in prison. It is the moral principle of the Freudian personality structure. It shows the behavior from the combination of all the interactions between personality structures.

Concept note-2: -Q. It is the deepest level in the mental iceberg and we are unaware of its contents. It is the moral principle of the Freudian personality structure. It is referred as the “little devil” wherein all negative aspects of the personality structure lives.

Concept note-3: -The top of the iceberg that you can see above the water represents the conscious mind. The part of the iceberg that is submerged below the water, but is still visible, is the preconscious. The bulk of the iceberg that lies unseen beneath the waterline represents the unconscious.

Concept note-4: -The unconscious mind (or the unconscious) consists of processes in the mind that occur automatically and are not available to introspection. Although these processes exist beneath the surface of conscious awareness, they are thought to exert an effect on conscious thought processes and behavior.

Concept note-5: -The superego is a part of the unconscious that is the voice of conscience (doing what is right) and the source of self-criticism.
