Which group of animals build nests to lay their hard eggs in?

(A) reptiles

(B) amphibians

(C) fish

(D) ** birds

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -You know that the Koel sings sweetly. Do you know that this bird does not make its own nest? It lays its eggs in a crow’s nest. The crow hatches them along with its own eggs.

Concept note-2: -cuckoo (Cuculus canorus) is a brood parasite; i.e., it lays its eggs in the nests of other birds, which act as foster parents for the young cuckoos. The most frequent foster parents are various species of small songbirds. Although the eggs of the various host species span a great range…

Concept note-3: -Birds live in shelters called nests. They use twigs, branches etc. to build them. The eggs are layed in nests. The weaver bird builds nests which are slightly different from nests of other birds.

Concept note-4: -The Brown-headed Cowbird (Molothrus ater) is a brood parasite, meaning that it lays its eggs in nests of other species.

Concept note-5: -Cuckoos or Koels don’t build their own nest. Instead they lay eggs in a crow’s nest.
