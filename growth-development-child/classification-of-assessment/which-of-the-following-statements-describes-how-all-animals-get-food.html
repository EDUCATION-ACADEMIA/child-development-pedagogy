Which of the following statements describes how all animals get food?

(A) ** Animals consume other organisms.

(B) Animals decompose dead plant matter.

(C) Animals hunt other animals in packs.

(D) Animals use light energy to make their own food.

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -All living things need food to stay alive, grow, and get energy. Nutrition is the process by which living things get or make food. All animals get food by eating other living things. Herbivores eat plants, while carnivores eat other animals.

Concept note-2: -Herbivores eat by grazing on plants or plucking the plant parts. Carnivores and insectivores have to hunt for their food. Animals like a cat, tiger, wolf, birds all have first to spot their prey and capture them. For this purpose, they often have to chase their prey.

Concept note-3: -A heterotroph is an organism that eats other plants or animals for energy and nutrients. The term stems from the Greek words hetero for “other” and trophe for “nourishment.” Organisms are characterized into two broad categories based upon how they obtain their energy and nutrients: autotrophs and heterotrophs.

Concept note-4: -Animals cannot make their own food so they must eat plants and/or other animals.
