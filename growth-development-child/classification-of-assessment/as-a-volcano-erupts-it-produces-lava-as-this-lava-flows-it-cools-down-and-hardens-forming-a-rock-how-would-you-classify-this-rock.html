As a volcano erupts, it produces lava. As this lava flows, it cools down and hardens, forming a rock. How would you classify this rock?

(A) as molten rock

(B) as a sedimentary

(C) as a metamorphic

(D) ** as an igneous rock

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -These are the rocks that form at erupting volcanoes and oozing fissures. The magma, called lava when molten rock erupts on the surface, cools and solidifies almost instantly when it is exposed to the relatively cool temperature of the atmosphere.

Concept note-2: -Lava cools to form volcanic rock as well as volcanic glass. Magma can also extrude into Earthâ€™s atmosphere as part of a violent volcanic explosion. This magma solidifies in the air to form volcanic rock called tephra. In the atmosphere, tephra is more often called volcanic ash.

Concept note-3: -When lava reaches the surface of the Earth through volcanoes or through great fissures the rocks that are formed from the lava cooling and hardening are called extrusive igneous rocks. Some of the more common types of extrusive igneous rocks are lava rocks, cinders, pumice, obsidian, and volcanic ash and dust.

Concept note-4: -Igneous rocks can be divided into four categories based on their chemical composition: felsic, intermediate, mafic, and ultramafic.

Concept note-5: -Igneous rocks form when molten rock (magma or lava) cools and solidifies.
