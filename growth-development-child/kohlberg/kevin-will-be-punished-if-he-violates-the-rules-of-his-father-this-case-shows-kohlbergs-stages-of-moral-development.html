GROWTH DEVELOPMENT CHILD

KOHLBERG

[SOURCES]
Kevin will be punished if he violates the rules of his father. This case shows Kohlberg’s stages of moral development____

(A) ** Stage 1: punishment and obedience orientation

(B) Stage 2: individualism and purpose

(C) Stage 3:norms-norma interpersonal

(D) Stage 4: the morality of the social system

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Level 1 (Preconventional) Obedience and Punishment Orientation – at this stage of moral reasoning, the individual acts from a belief that if they do something wrong they will be punished, so that the best reason for avoiding doing wrong is to avoid punishment.

Concept note-2: -Postconventional morality is the third stage of moral development, and is characterized by an individuals’ understanding of universal ethical principles. These are abstract and ill-defined, but might include: the preservation of life at all costs, and the importance of human dignity.

Concept note-3: -Kohlberg calls stage 1 thinking “preconventional” because children do not yet speak as members of society. Instead, they see morality as something external to themselves, as that which the big people say they must do.

Concept note-4: -Stage 4 (Maintaining Social Order): This stage is focused on ensuring that social order is maintained. At this stage of moral development, people begin to consider society as a whole when making judgments. The focus is on maintaining law and order by following the rules, doing one’s duty, and respecting authority.
