One of your friends tells you to steal some candy. You are in the first level 1, why are you NOT stealing?

(A) I wouldn’t like it either if someone stole something from me

(B) It’s against the law

(C) If everyone stole, the store would have to close

(D) ** I could have problems

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Stealing may be caused by jealousy, low self-esteem, or peer-pressure. Social issues like feeling excluded or overlooked can also cause stealing. People may steal to prove their independence, to act out against family or friends, or because they don’t respect others or themselves.

Concept note-2: -How do I tell a “friend stealer” that her actions are hurtful? Just tell her how you feel. If she doesn’t want to be your friend, make new ones. If the friend stealer keeps following you and steals your new friends, tell her why she’s not hanging out with your friend.

Concept note-3: -Do not ask him if he stole the item; that will only invite him to lie. Instead, let him know you are aware that he took the item, and that you expect him to return it and to apologize to the owner of the item. If the student no longer has the item, tell him you expect him to replace it or pay for it.
