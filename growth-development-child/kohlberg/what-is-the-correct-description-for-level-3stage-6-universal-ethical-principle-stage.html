What is the correct description for Level 3:Stage 6 (“Universal Ethical Principle” Stage)?

(A) ** Laws are viewed as valid as long as they promote justice. People in this stage have their own sense of right and wrong.

(B) People in this stage blindly accept rules because rules help maintain society.

(C) People in this stage believe that rules and laws are in place to help protect everyone’s rights.

(D) This stage focuses on the child’s desire to obey rules and avoid being punished.

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Stage 6: Universal ethical principle orientation These principles are abstract and universal in application. This type of reasoning involves taking the perspective of every person or group that could potentially be affected by the decision.

Concept note-2: -Stage 3-“good boy-good girl” orientation, This stage of moral development is focused on living up to social expectations and roles. There is an emphasis on conformity, being “nice, ” and consideration of how choices influence relationships.

Concept note-3: -Level 3-Postconventional morality Postconventional morality is the third stage of moral development, and is characterized by an individuals’ understanding of universal ethical principles. These are abstract and ill-defined, but might include: the preservation of life at all costs, and the importance of human dignity.

Concept note-4: -There were three levels of moral reasoning that encompassed the six stages. Like Piaget, subjects were unlikely to regress in their moral development, but instead, moved forward through the stages: pre-conventional, conventional, and finally post-conventional.
