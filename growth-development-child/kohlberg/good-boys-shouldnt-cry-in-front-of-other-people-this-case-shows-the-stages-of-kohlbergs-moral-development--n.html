GROWTH DEVELOPMENT CHILD

KOHLBERG

[SOURCES]
Good boys shouldn’t cry in front of other people. This case shows the stages of Kohlberg’s moral development ____ n

(A) Stage 3:norms-norma interpersonal

(B) Stage 4: the morality of the social system

(C) ** Stage 5 community rights vs individual rights

(D) Stage 6: universal ethical principles

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -At stage 5, people begin to ask, “What makes for a good society?” They begin to think about society in a very theoretical way, stepping back from their own society and considering the rights and values that a society ought to uphold. They then evaluate existing societies in terms of these prior considerations.

Concept note-2: -Stage 5: Social Contract and Individual Rights This stage acknowledges the introduction of abstract reasoning as people attempt to explain specific behaviors. In our example above, the man should steal the medication for his wife because she is deathly ill and the laws do not take the circumstances into account.

Concept note-3: -Kohlberg identified three distinct levels of moral reasoning: preconventional, conventional, and postconventional.

Concept note-4: -According to Kohlberg’s theory of moral development, the first stage is the punishment and obedience orientation. The second stage is the instrumental relativist orientation. Good boy-nice girl orientation is the third stage. Society-maintaining orientation stage is the fourth stage.
