It supposes an attitude shared by the community and concerned with the “good reasons” of society.

(A) preconventional

(B) ** Conventional

(C) Postconvencional

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Culture consists of the beliefs, behaviors, objects, and other characteristics common to the members of a particular group or society. Through culture, people and groups define themselves, conform to society’s shared values, and contribute to society.

Concept note-2: -A culture is a way of life of a group of people–the behaviors, beliefs, values, and symbols that they accept, generally without thinking about them, and that are passed along by communication and imitation from one generation to the next.

Concept note-3: -Cultural relativism is the attitude that a society’s customs and ideas should be viewed within the context of that society’s problems and opportunities.

Concept note-4: -Nonmaterial culture, in contrast, consists of the ideas, attitudes, and beliefs of a society.
