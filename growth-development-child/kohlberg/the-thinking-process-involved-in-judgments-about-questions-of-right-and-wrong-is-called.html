KOHLBERG

[SOURCES]
The thinking process involved in judgments about questions of right and wrong is called

(A) Distributive justice

(B) Perspective-taking ability

(C) ** Moral reasoning

(D) Moral realism

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Hence, it becomes clear that according to Kohlberg, the thinking process involved in judgements about questions of right & wrong is called ‘Moral dilemma’.

Concept note-2: -According to Kohlberg moral dilemma is the thinking process involved in judgments about questions of right & wrong.

Concept note-3: -According to Kohlberg, the thinking process involved in judgements about questions of right and wrong is called a moral dilemma. Kohlberg gave his theory based on his work with children, he interviewed them and presented the dilemmas, based on the responses he gave the stages of moral development in children.

Concept note-4: -Kohlberg (1963) built on the work of Piaget and was interested in finding out how our moral reasoning changes as we get older. He wanted to find out how people decide what is right and what is wrong.
