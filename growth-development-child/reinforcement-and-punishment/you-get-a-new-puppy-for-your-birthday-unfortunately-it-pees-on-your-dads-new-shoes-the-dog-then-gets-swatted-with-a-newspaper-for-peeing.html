GROWTH DEVELOPMENT CHILD

REINFORCEMENT AND PUNISHMENT

[SOURCES]
You get a new puppy for your birthday. Unfortunately it pees on your Dad’s new shoes. The dog then gets swatted with a newspaper for peeing

(A) Positive Reinforcement

(B) Negative Reinforcement

(C) ** Positive Punishment

(D) Negative Punishment

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Water, food, sleep, shelter, sex, and touch, among others, are primary reinforcers. Pleasure is also a primary reinforcer.

Concept note-2: -Explanation: In operant conditioning, “negative” and “positive” refer to whether an unpleasant stimulus is taken away or a pleasant stimulus is added, respectively. “Reinforcement” increases the likelihood a behavior will occur, while “punishment” decreases the likelihood a behavior will occur.

Concept note-3: -Answer and Explanation: The correct answer is C. When a dog plays dead she gets a treat in order to encourage her to repeat the behavior. The dog has learned that she will get a treat, positive reinforcement, for playing dead in this example.

Concept note-4: -Behaviorism assumes humans are like animals, ignores the internal cognitive processes that underlie behavior, and focuses solely on changes in observable behavior. From a behaviorist perspective, the role of the learner is to be acted upon by the teacher-controlled environment.
