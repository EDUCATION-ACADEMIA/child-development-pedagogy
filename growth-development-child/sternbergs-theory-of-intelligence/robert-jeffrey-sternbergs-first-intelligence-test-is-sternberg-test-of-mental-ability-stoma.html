STERNBERGS THEORY OF INTELLIGENCE

[SOURCES]
Robert Jeffrey Sternberg’s first intelligence test is Sternberg Test of Mental Ability (STOMA)

(A) ** True

(B) false

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Sternberg argued that practical intelligence-a person’s ability to react and adapt to the world around them-as well as creativity are equally important when measuring an individual’s overall intelligence. He also argued that intelligence isn’t fixed, but rather comprises a set of abilities that can be developed.

Concept note-2: -Sternberg’s theory identifies three types of intelligence: practical, creative, and analytical. Practical intelligence, as proposed by Sternberg, is sometimes compared to “street smarts.” Being practical means you find solutions that work in your everyday life by applying knowledge based on your experiences.

Concept note-3: -In keeping with the latter theoretical support, the Sternberg Triarchic Abilities Test (STAT) is an intelligence test that is designed to measure three distinct types of intelligence: analytical, practical, and creative.

Concept note-4: -A successfully intelligent person accomplishes these goals by figuring out his or her strengths and weaknesses, and then by capitalizing on the strengths and correcting or compensating for the weaknesses. Strengths and weaknesses are in terms of four kinds of skills: creative, analytical, practical, and wisdom-based.
