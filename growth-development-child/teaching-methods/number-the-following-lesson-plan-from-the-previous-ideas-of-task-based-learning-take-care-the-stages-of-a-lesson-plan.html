TEACHING METHODS

[SOURCES]
NUMBER THE FOLLOWING LESSON PLAN FROM THE PREVIOUS IDEAS OF TASK BASED LEARNING TAKE CARE THE STAGES OF A LESSON PLAN

(A) ** Pre-task (15-20min)Aim:To introduce the topic of nights out and to give the class exposure to language related to it. To highlight words and phrases. Show sts pictures of a night out in a restaurant / bar and ask them where they go to have a good night out.

(B) Planning (10min) Each pair rehearses presenting their night out. Teacher walks around, helps them if they need it and notes down any language points to be highlighted later.

(C) Task (10min) Students do the task in twos and plan the night. Match them with another pair to discuss their ideas and any similarities and differences.

(D) Preparation and materialsYou will need to record two people planning a night out on the town.

(E) Report (15 min) Class listen to the plans, their task is to choose one of them. They can ask questions after the presentation.

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Task-based learning offers an alternative for language teachers. In a task-based lesson the teacher doesnâ€™t pre-determine what language will be studied, the lesson is based around the completion of a central task and the language studied is determined by what happens as the students complete it.

Concept note-2: -What is the aim of a task-based course? In task-based learning, a lesson or unit is designed to help learners to complete a task: for example; write a letter, make a reservation, plan a trip, collaborate to design something, or hold a meeting.

Concept note-3: -In essence, a language lesson based on the Task-Based Language Teaching method has three stages, the Pre-Task Activity, the Task and the Wrap-up or Review.
