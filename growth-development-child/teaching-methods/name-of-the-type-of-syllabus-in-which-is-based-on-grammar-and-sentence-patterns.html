Name of the type of syllabus in which is based on grammar and sentence patterns

(A) Situational

(B) ** Structural

(C) Topical

(D) Situational

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -A grammatical syllabus is based on the structures of a language. It can be compared to other types of syllabi based around tasks, vocabulary, functions or topics.

Concept note-2: -A structural syllabus consists of a list of grammatical items, usually. arranged in the order in which they are to be taught. This kind of syllabus is probably still the most common in language teaching today.

Concept note-3: -In language teaching contexts, there are six types of language syllabus: grammar syllabus, situational syllabus, notional syllabus, task-based syllabus, skill-based syllabus and content-based syllabus.

Concept note-4: -There are two major types of syllabuses, product-oriented syllabus and process-oriented syllabus. A good and valid syllabus is that covers more or less all aspects of both these types, therefore, proper and appropriate implementation of syllabus in language teaching is undeniable.

Concept note-5: -Structural syllabus, also known as the grammatical syllabus, is the most common and traditional syllabus type. It has been in use by language teachers for many years.
