GROWTH DEVELOPMENT CHILD

TEACHING METHODS

[SOURCES]
Teaching method that encourages co-operation instead of competition so that the learners benefit from sharing knowledge and information.

(A) ** Collaborative Learning

(B) Communicative Language Teaching

(C) Activity-Based Language Teaching

(D) Natural Method

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Cooperative learning is a strategy used within groups of learners and aims to improve their learning experience and understanding of a learning subject. This type of learning strategy uses small group tasks and activities as a learning experience.

Concept note-2: -Peer learning, or peer instruction, is a type of collaborative learning that involves students working in pairs or small groups to discuss concepts or find solutions to problems.

Concept note-3: -What is it? A collaborative (or cooperative) learning approach involves pupils working together on activities or learning tasks in a group small enough to ensure that everyone participates. Pupils in the group may work on separate tasks contributing to a common overall outcome, or work together on a shared task.

Concept note-4: -The process of cooperative learning involves students working together in small groups on a structured activity. The members of the groups learn to work as a team to accomplish a specific goal, to solve a problem, to complete a project, or to develop a product.

Concept note-5: -An effective cooperative learning strategy to implement peer tutoring in class is Rally Coach. In pairs, students take turns, one student solving problems while talking through their thinking aloud, while the other listens, coaches where necessary and provides positive feedback.
