TEACHING METHODS

[SOURCES]
Emphasis on spoken than written language

(A) ** Audiolingual Method

(B) Grammar Translation Method

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Hence, it is clear that the Audio Lingual Method emphasizes more on spoken than written language. Grammar-Translation Method: In this method, the learner first learns grammatical rules and then applies those rules in translating sentences from the target language in the mother tongue.

Concept note-2: -Audio-lingualism is a method of foreign language teaching where the emphasis is on learning grammatical and phonological structure, especially for speaking and listening. It is based on behaviourism and so relies on formation as a basis for learning, through a great deal of mechanical repetition.

Concept note-3: -Spoken language is seen as the natural productive tool among people that is more common and normal, whereas written language is seen as being intricate and complicated due to its extensive rules.

Concept note-4: -Audio-lingual approach suggests that students be taught phonology, morphology, and syntax of the language; all these patterns can be learned through contrastive analysis of the differences between the native tongue and the target language, which helps students to acquire new language easier.
