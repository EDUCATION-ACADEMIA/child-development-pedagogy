GROWTH DEVELOPMENT CHILD

TEACHING METHODS

[SOURCES]
This is a method which is based on the coordination of speech and action.

(A) ** Total Physical Response Method

(B) Direct method

(C) Grammar Translation Method

(D) Communicative Approach

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Total Physical Response (TPR) is a language teaching method built around the coordination of speech and action; it attempts to teach language through physical (motor) activity.

Concept note-2: -Total Physical Response (TPR) is a method of teaching language or vocabulary concepts by using physical movement to react to verbal input. The process mimics the way that infants learn their first language, and it reduces student inhibitions and lowers stress.

Concept note-3: -In this method, the teacher gives commands to students and they will follow them. It is a language teaching method based on the coordination of language and physical development.

Concept note-4: -Total Physical Response is a language teaching method which is based on the assumption that the coordination of speech and action will boost language learning. It was developed by James Asher in the 70s. He drew from a variety of areas, including psychology, learning theory and humanistic pedagogy.

Concept note-5: -Hence, it could be concluded that in Total Physical Response as a method, listening comprehension is the basis of learning.
