DYSLEXIA

[SOURCES]
A(n) ____ syllable is a word or part of a word that ends in at least one consonant after one vowel. The vowel makes a short sound.

(A) open

(B) r-controlled

(C) vowel-consonant e

(D) ** closed

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The nucleus ‘N’ of the syllable includes the peak and an off-set ‘Off’ to the peak where the semivowels occur. The nucleus may also contain an onset to the peak where a glide may occur. The next higher unit is called the rhyme ‘R’.

Concept note-2: -A syllable is a unit of organization for a sequence of speech sounds typically made up of a syllable nucleus (most often a vowel) with optional initial and final margins (typically, consonants).

Concept note-3: -A syllable is a single, unbroken sound of a spoken (or written) word. Syllables usually contain a vowel and accompanying consonants. Sometimes syllables are referred to as the ‘beats’ of spoken language.

Concept note-4: -A syllable that ends with a vowel sound is called an open syllable, and a syllable that ends with a consonant sound is called a closed syllable.
