DYSLEXIA

[SOURCES]
Dyslexia programming should be ____ based.

(A) Reading Success

(B) Research Based

(C) ** Orton Gillingham

(D) Three D

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Many reading programs include Orton–Gillingham ideas, including a “multisensory” approach, which is considered highly effective for teaching students with dyslexia.

Concept note-2: -Orton–Gillingham The Orton-Gillingham approach is the “gold standard” for teaching reading to kids with dyslexia. It focuses at the word level by teaching the connections between letters and sounds. Orton–Gillingham also uses what’s called a multisensory approach.

Concept note-3: -A review of 24 studies on the Orton-Gillingham method, published in the July 2021 issue of the peer-reviewed journal Exceptional Children, found no statistically significant benefit for children with dyslexia.

Concept note-4: -Orton–Gillingham is a teaching approach that was designed to help struggling readers. It explicitly teaches the connections between letters and sounds. Many reading programs include Orton–Gillingham ideas. Orton–Gillingham is a structured literacy approach.
