DYSLEXIA

[SOURCES]
Dyslexia is caused by poor instruction?

(A) True

(B) ** False

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Dyslexia results from individual differences in the parts of the brain that enable reading. It tends to run in families. Dyslexia appears to be linked to certain genes that affect how the brain processes reading and language.

Concept note-2: -Dyslexic people can struggle with direction: they may often get lost or feel nervous about going to unfamiliar places. They may also find ‘left’ or ‘right’ instructions difficult to follow, or give.

Concept note-3: -Evidence-based reading instruction for dyslexia must include all 5 components outlined by the National Reading Panel. These 5 components are phonemic awareness, systematic phonics, fluency, vocabulary, and comprehension instruction.

Concept note-4: -Dyslexia is not the result of lack of motivation, sensory impairment, inadequate instructional or environmental opportunities, or other limiting conditions, but may occur together with these conditions.
