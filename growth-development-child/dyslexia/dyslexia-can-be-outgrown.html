DYSLEXIA

[SOURCES]
Dyslexia can be outgrown.

(A) Fact

(B) ** Myth

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -As noted above, dyslexia is a lifelong condition. The good news is, with proper intervention, the majority of dyslexics become proficient readers and lead successful lives.

Concept note-2: -People do not outgrow dyslexia, although the symptoms do tend to vary by age. With appropriate instruction and support, people with dyslexia can succeed in school and the workplace. Keep reading to learn more about how dyslexia can affect people at different ages.

Concept note-3: -There’s no known cure for dyslexia, but there are ways to help manage it. “While dyslexia may look different at various stages of life, it doesn’t just go away over time, ” says Dr. Kahn, “but there are ways to help people with dyslexia improve their skills and be more successful in reading tasks.”

Concept note-4: -When dyslexia develops later in life tied to brain illness or injury, it is called “acquired dyslexia”-the symptoms may very well be the same, but the underlying cause is different.

Concept note-5: -Dyslexia is not a curse it’s just the ability of world to think of people as being different, we hope as time goes by that dyslexia is thought about more as a positive than a negative.
