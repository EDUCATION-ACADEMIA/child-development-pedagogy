GROWTH DEVELOPMENT CHILD

COGNITIVE THEORIES

[SOURCES]
Your students will undoubtedly know a great many things and will not be able to show you everything they know. According to social cognitive theorists, your students will be most likely to show you:

(A) things they have learned at a concrete rather than abstract level

(B) things they are especially excited about

(C) ** things they think they will be reinforced for demonstrating

(D) things they learned recently rather than a long time ago

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Should students ever be allowed to fail at classroom tasks? According to social cognitive theorists, the answer is: Yes. Occasional failures interspersed among frequent successes teach students that perseverance pays off.

Concept note-2: –Social-cognitive theorists believe we learn many of our behaviors either through conditioning or by observing and imitating others.-They also emphasize the importance of mental processes: What we think about a situation affects our resulting behavior.

Concept note-3: -What is Standards based education? The Standards define what students should know and be able to do at each level. The Standards can be used as a reference point for planning teaching and learning programs, and for assessing student progress.

Concept note-4: -Which one of the following statements best illustrates social cognitive theorists’ notion of reciprocal causation? The environment affects students’ behavior, but their behavior also affects the environment they experience.
