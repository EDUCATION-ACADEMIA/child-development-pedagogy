According to social cognitive theorists, should students ever be allowed to fail at classroom task?

(A) No. Even a single failure can seriously deflate students self-efficacy

(B) No. Failure gives students the message that school is not an enjoyable place to be

(C) Yes. Self-efficacy is highest when students fail at a task several times in a row before accomplishing it successfully

(D) ** yes. Occasional failures interspersed among frequent successes teach students that perseverance pays off.

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Should students ever be allowed to fail at classroom tasks? According to social cognitive theorists, the answer is: Yes. Occasional failures interspersed among frequent successes teach students that perseverance pays off.

Concept note-2: -Should students ever be allowed to fail at classroom tasks? If we take into account the concept of resilient self-efficacy, the answer is:-Yes. Self-efficacy is highest when students fail at a task several times in a row before accomplishing it successfully.

Concept note-3: -In social cognitive theory (SCT; Bandura, 1982), behavior is held to be determined by four factors: goals, outcome expectancies, self-efficacy, and sociostructural variables.

Concept note-4: -One of the main criticisms of the social cognitive theory is that it is not a unified theory. This means that the different aspects of the theory may not be connected. For example, researchers currently cannot find a connection between observational learning and self-efficacy within the social-cognitive perspective.
