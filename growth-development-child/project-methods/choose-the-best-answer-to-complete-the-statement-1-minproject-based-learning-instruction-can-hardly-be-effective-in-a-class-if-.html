Choose the best answer to complete the statement. (1 min)Project-based learning instruction can hardly be effective in a class if ____

(A) the school principal fully embraces it.

(B) the other subject teachers in the school fully embrace it.

(C) the teacher of that class has time to plan quality PBL units.

(D) ** the goal is to be in the top 3 schools at the National University Entrance Test.

(E) the project is shared with students of another school

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Project-based learning (PBL) involves students designing, developing, and constructing hands-on solutions to a problem. The educational value of PBL is that it aims to build studentsâ€™ creative capacity to work through difficult or ill-structured problems, commonly in small teams.

Concept note-2: -It promotes inquiry skills, enhances problem-solving skills and establishes interdisciplinary linkages. Thus from above-mentioned points, it is clear that Projects make scoring easy in Mathematics is not true.

Concept note-3: -PBL is more effective than traditional methods (based mainly on lectures) at improving social and communication skills, problem-solving and self-learning skills, and has no worse results (and in many studies better results) in relation to academic performance.

Concept note-4: -Embedding project-based learning in Advanced Placement courses increased the probability of students earning a passing score on AP tests by about 8 percentage points in the first year and 10 percentage points after teachers had two years of experience with the project-based curriculum (Saavedra, Liu, et al., 2021).
