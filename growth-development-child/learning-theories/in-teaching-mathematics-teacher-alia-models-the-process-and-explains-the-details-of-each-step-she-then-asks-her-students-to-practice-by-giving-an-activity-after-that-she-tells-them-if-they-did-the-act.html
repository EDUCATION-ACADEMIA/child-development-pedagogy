In teaching mathematics, Teacher Alia models the process and explains the details of each step. She then asks her students to practice by giving an activity. After that, she tells them if they did the activity correctly or not. This approach is an example of:

(A) ** direct instruction

(B) interactive instruction

(C) experimental instruction

(D) independent instruction

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -In its broadest sense, mathematical modeling is the process of a problem solving by the mathematical expression of real life event or a problem. This process enables learners to relate mathematics to real life and to learn it more meaningfully and permanently.

Concept note-2: -The Process Model of Curriculum Development A curriculum that has been developed using the process model will often prioritize active learning, discussion, and individual student response. The thinking behind this model is that students will learn a significant amount by engaging in activities and discussions.
