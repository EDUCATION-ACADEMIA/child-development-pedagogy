Bruner’s three stages of cognitive representation follow which order, from earliest to lates?

(A) Iconic, Symbolic, Enactive

(B) ** Enactive, Iconic, Symbolic

(C) Symbolic, Enactive, Iconic

(D) Enactive, Symbolic, Iconic

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Jerome Bruner identified three stages of cognitive representation. Enactive, which is the representation of knowledge through actions. Iconic, which is the visual summarization of images. Symbolic representation, which is the use of words and other symbols to describe experiences.

Concept note-2: -Jerome Bruner’s Theory of Development is based on the assumption that we learn best when we go from concrete to abstract in a three-step process: First comes hands-on “Action”, then learning with “Images” and finally students transform what they’ve learned into “Language”.

Concept note-3: -In Bruner’s research of cognitive development of children in 1966, he proposed three modes of representations-enactive, iconic, and symbolic. This type of representation happens in the very young (birth to age 1). It involves encoding action based information that is then stored into our memory.

Concept note-4: -Hence, it could be concluded that the right order of three modes of representation proposed by Jerome S Bruner is Enactive representation leading to Iconic representation and finally Symbolic representation.
