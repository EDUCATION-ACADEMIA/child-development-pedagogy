Characteristics of a Social Constructivist learning

(A) Students respond in the desired way

(B) Students follow teachers instructions

(C) ** Groups discussion and problem solving

(D) Learning can be independent of the teacher

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Social constructivism focuses on the collaborative nature of learning. Knowledge develops from how people interact with each other, their culture, and society at large. Students rely on others to help create their building blocks, and learning from others helps them construct their own knowledge and reality.

Concept note-2: -Characteristics of Socio-constructivist classroom: Children discussing while working in groups is a characteristic of an effective socio-constructivist classroom that caters to the needs of individual children as it ensures the active involvement of learners and promotes peer tutoring.

Concept note-3: -New challenges and problems often emerge through the process. The constructivist approach relies on the process of evolving problem solving to create critical thinking skills as part of the newly constructed knowledge set.
