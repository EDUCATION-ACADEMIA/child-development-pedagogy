(C) ** ´Team sports many performers could be said to automatically react to the sound of the whistle blown by a referee

(D) ** This theory supports the importance of learning from the environment highlighting the nurture over nature approach and philosophy

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Classical conditioning emphasizes the importance of learning from the environment, and supports nurture over nature. However, it is limiting to describe behavior solely in terms of either nature or nurture, and attempts to do this underestimate the complexity of human behavior.

Concept note-2: -Classical conditioning definition Classical conditioning is a type of learning that happens unconsciously. When you learn through classical conditioning, an automatic conditioned response is paired with a specific stimulus. This creates a behavior.

Concept note-3: -Classical conditioning is a behaviorist theory of learning. It posits that when a naturally occurring stimulus and an environmental stimulus are repeatedly paired, the environmental stimulus will eventually elicit a similar response to the natural stimulus.

Concept note-4: -Classical conditioning explains many aspects of human behavior. It plays an important role in generating emotional responses, advertising, addiction, psychotherapy, hunger etc. Classical conditioning also finds its application at school, post traumatic disorders or associating something with the past.
