When John turns on the light to his aquarium he sprinkles food into the water and the fish swim to the top. What is the unconditioned stimulus?

(A) ** the food

(B) the water

(C) the light

(D) John

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Examples of Unconditioned Stimuli The smell of a favorite food, which immediately makes you feel hungry. A feather tickling your nose, which causes you to sneeze. An onion’s smell as you cut it, which makes your eyes water.

Concept note-2: -An unconditioned stimulus is a stimulus that leads to an automatic response. In Pavlov’s experiment, the food was the unconditioned stimulus. An unconditioned response is an automatic response to a stimulus. The dogs salivating for food is the unconditioned response in Pavlov’s experiment.

Concept note-3: -During conditioning, the unconditioned stimulus (food) is presented repeatedly just after the presentation of the neutral stimulus (bell). After conditioning, the neutral stimulus alone produces a conditioned response (salivation), thus becoming a conditioned stimulus.

Concept note-4: -Some more examples of unconditioned responses include: Gasping in pain after being stung by a bee. Jerking your hand back after touching a hot plate on the oven. Jumping at the sound of a loud noise.
