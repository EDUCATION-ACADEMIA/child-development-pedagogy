LEARNING THEORIES

Bandora proposed that (choose two)

(A) ** Aggression was more likely to be imitated if children saw an adult model get rewarded for doing so

(B) ** Aggression was a learnt behaviour like anything else

(C) Aggression was less likely to be imitated if children saw the adult model get rewarded for doing so

(D) Aggression was innate

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Bandura proposed that aggression can also be learnt by the indirect mechanism of observational learning. Social learning theory maintains that children learn through a process of imitation. Aggressive acts carried out by a role model will be internalised by an individual and reproduced in the future.

Concept note-2: -Albert Bandura’s social learning theory suggests that observation and modeling play a primary role in how and why people learn. Bandura’s theory goes beyond the perception of learning being the result of direct experience with the environment.

Concept note-3: -Bandura asserts that most human behavior is learned through observation, imitation, and modeling.

Concept note-4: -Social learning theories suggest that children learn to exhibit aggressive behaviors because they observe others acting aggressively and can see how these behaviors are reinforced over time (Bandura, 1973).
