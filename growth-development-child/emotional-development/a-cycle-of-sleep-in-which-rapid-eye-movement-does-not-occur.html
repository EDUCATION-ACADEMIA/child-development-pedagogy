A cycle of sleep in which rapid eye movement does not occur.

(A) ** NREM Sleep

(B) REM Sleep

(C) Nightmare

(D) Sleep-deprived

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Stages N1 to N3 are considered non-rapid eye movement (NREM) sleep, with each stage a progressively deeper sleep.

Concept note-2: -The eye movements occur during all stages of NREM sleep and can occur with other SSRIs, such as Paroxetine, Sertraline, and Citalopram.

Concept note-3: -NREM sleep is differentiated from rapid eye movement (REM) sleep. View Source because sleepers experience slowed eye movements during NREM sleep. Stage 1 and stage 2 NREM sleep are considered light sleep, while stage 3 NREM sleep is considered deep sleep.

Concept note-4: -As you sleep, your body cycles through non-REM and REM sleep. You usually start the sleep cycle with stage 1 of non-REM sleep. You pass through the other stages of non-REM sleep, followed by a short period of REM sleep.

Concept note-5: -Which of the following does not occur during REM (rapid eye movement) sleep? Explanation: During rapid eye movement (REM) sleep, the random movement of the eyes is not voluntary because the person is soundly sleeping.
