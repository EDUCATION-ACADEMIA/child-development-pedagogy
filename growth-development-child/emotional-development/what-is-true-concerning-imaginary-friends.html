What is true concerning imaginary friends?

(A) ** Imaginary friends usually fade away at about age five.

(B) Most children begin having imaginary friends at about age six.

(C) Typically only children who have no real friends have imaginary friends.

(D) Imaginary friends usually fade away at about age nine.

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -In some cases, the friend doesn’t truly go away, but the child will stop talking about him for fear of being made fun of. If your kid holds on to her imaginary friend for longer, there isn’t reason to worry. “It’s completely fine, ” Dr.

Concept note-2: -The bottom line Having an imaginary friend is a normal and healthy part of childhood play. Having one has even shown benefits in childhood development. If your child has an imaginary friend, it’s totally OK. They can grow out of it in their own time as they stop needing the skills that their companion is teaching them.

Concept note-3: -How long do imaginary friends last? An imaginary friend can be present in a child’s live for anywhere from a few months to a few years. For most children, imaginary friends taper off by late elementary school-around age 8 or 9. For a few kids, though, invisible friends can last much longer, even into the teen years.

Concept note-4: -Imaginary friends are a common-and normal-manifestation for many kids across many stages of development. In fact, by age 7, 65 percent of children will have had an imaginary friend, according to a 2004 study.

Concept note-5: -They are, according to some children, physically indistinguishable from real people, while others say they see their imaginary friends only in their heads, and still others cannot see the friend at all but can sense his/her presence.
