Which of these is not encouraged by a baby’s development of attachment?

(A) Self-esteem

(B) ** Anxiety

(C) Trust

(D) Social skills

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The major determinant of the infant’s pattern of attachment (secure, avoidant, ambivalent, or disorganized) appears to be the quality of care the primary caregiver(s) provides.

Concept note-2: -Bonding and attachment happen when you consistently and warmly respond to your baby’s needs for safety, attention and stimulation. Smiling, playing, talking and cuddling are all good ways to bond with your baby. As your baby develops, you’ll find new ways to bond.

Concept note-3: -Bowlby specified four phases of child-caregiver attachment development: 0-3 months, 3-6 months, 6 months to 3 years, and 3 years through the end of childhood. Expanding on Bowlby’s ideas, Mary Ainsworth pointed to three attachment patterns: secure attachment, avoidant attachment, and resistant attachment.
