What age does the child develop self-confidence and self-esteem?

(A) ** 6

(B) 5

(C) 4

(D) 2

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Our research has developed new ways to study what kids think about themselves. Parents, make a note: our results show that most kids develop a sense of self-esteem – feeling good or bad about oneself – as early as age five, before they even enter kindergarten.

Concept note-2: -Self-esteem first begins to rise between ages 4 and 11, as children develop socially and cognitively and gain some sense of independence. Levels then seem to plateau-but not decline-as the teenage years begin from ages 11 to 15, the data show.

Concept note-3: -Talk about themselves in a very negative way “I’m stupid”, “I’m ugly” Constantly compare themselves to others in a negative way. Become overly concerned about other people’s opinion of them.

Concept note-4: -Children develop self-esteem by age 5, much earlier than previously thought, according to new research that suggests children gain either a positive or negative view of themselves before they begin formal schooling. The researchers found that self-esteem at age 5 is as strong as those measured in adults.

Concept note-5: -Self-esteem means you mostly feel good about yourself. Kids with self-esteem: feel proud of what they can do. see the good things about themselves. believe in themselves, even when they don’t do well at first.
