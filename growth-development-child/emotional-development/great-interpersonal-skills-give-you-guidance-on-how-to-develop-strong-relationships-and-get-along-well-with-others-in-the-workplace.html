EMOTIONAL DEVELOPMENT

[SOURCES]
Great interpersonal skills give you guidance on how to develop strong relationships and get along well with others in the workplace.

(A) ** True

(B) False

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Good interpersonal skills help you to communicate more effectively with family and friends. This is likely to be particularly important with your partner. For example, being able to give and receive feedback effectively with your partner can help to resolve small problems between you before they become big issues.

Concept note-2: -Good interpersonal communication drives more employee recognition. When employees have good interpersonal relationships with each other and their managers, they are more likely to recognize each others’ good work and give constructive feedback.

Concept note-3: -respect-always be considerate, honest and value others’ opinions. openness-be aware of the different backgrounds and perspectives of your colleagues. effective communication-know when to use different methods of communication (e.g. private meeting, group discussion, email, letter etc.).

Concept note-4: -Interpersonal skills are the behaviours you use every day to interact and communicate with others well. Interpersonal skills encompass a wide range of skills, but some of the most common are active listening, conflict resolution, communication, collaboration and emotional intelligence.

Concept note-5: -Interpersonal communication skills can improve your personal and professional relationships by helping you to express your thoughts and convey your intent clearly. Additionally, you’ll be more able to understand and empathise with others due to your listening skills.
