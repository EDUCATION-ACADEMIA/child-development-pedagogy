Three-year-old Ali says, “I have five cars. I look good. I drew a house and a family in the nursery today.” This demonstrates that Ali is beginning to develop

(A) self-confidence

(B) self-esteem

(C) self-conciseness

(D) ** self-concept

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Around the same time children demonstrate self-recognition, they begin to use words such as “I” and “mine.”3 Children continue to develop self-concept as they demonstrate an awareness of their own characteristics and begin to identify their own feelings and preferences in everyday interactions.

Concept note-2: -According to Erikson the psychological conflict of the preschool years is initiative vs. guilt. Resolved positively through social experiences that foster a healthy sense of initiative & through development of superego, or conscience, that is not too strict, by identifying w/ same-sex parent.

Concept note-3: -Your 3 year old can understand 1000 or more words. They can understand ‘place’ words – under, on, beside, back, over. They’ll ask lots of ‘What’, ‘Who’, ‘Where’ and ‘Why’ questions. They can talk about what happened yesterday and about tomorrow.

Concept note-4: -Most children will have dips in self-esteem as they go through different stages or challenges in life, and there are different pressures that may affect them-including social media, bullying, exams, family problems and abuse.
