Who was the researcher who found the limitation of the Tri-component model?

(A) ** La Pierre

(B) Zimbardo

(C) Freud

(D) Piaget

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The rationale of this study is based on the tricomponent attitude model proposed by Rosenberg and Hanland (1960a), which explains consumer attitude formation according to a three-dimension psychological process.

Concept note-2: -Limitations of the Tri-Component Model of Attitudes Some psychologists believe that there are possible only affective and cognitive components of attitudes because a person’s behaviour does not always reflect the attitude they hold. In other words, the behavioural component is often inconsistent.

Concept note-3: -structure, process and time play important roles in the origin and development of landforms of a particular place. These three factors are called as ‘Trio of Davis’ and his concept is expressed as follows: “Landscape is a function of structure, process and time” (also called as stages by Davis’ followers).

Concept note-4: -LaPiere had a longstanding research agenda of studying how race and ethnicity impacted both social norms and social psychology.
