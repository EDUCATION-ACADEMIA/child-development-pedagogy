GROWTH DEVELOPMENT CHILD

BRONFENBRENNERS ECOLOGICAL SYSTEMS

[SOURCES]
What is the most influential level of the ecological systems theory?

(A) Chronosystem

(B) Exosystem

(C) ** microsystem

(D) macrosystem

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -A microsystem typically includes family, peers, or caregivers. Relationships in a microsystem are bi-directional. In other words, your reactions to the people in your microsystem will affect how they treat you in return. This is the most influential level of the ecological systems theory.

Concept note-2: -The microsystem is the most inuential level of the ecological systems theory. This is the most immediate environmental settings containing the developing child, such as family and school.

Concept note-3: -The microsystem has the most direct influence on childhood development and includes a child’s family, peers, and school.

Concept note-4: -1. Why is the microsystem the most influential environmental level in a person’s life? Because it is the system where a person has the most direct contact with factors influencing development. Because it involves a person’s cultural environment.

Concept note-5: -Bronfenbrenner’s structure of environment: The microsystem – this is the layer closest to the child and contains the structures with which the child has direct contact. The microsystem encompasses the relationships and interactions a child has with her immediate surroundings (Berk, 2000).
