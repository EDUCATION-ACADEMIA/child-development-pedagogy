GROWTH DEVELOPMENT CHILD

BRONFENBRENNERS ECOLOGICAL SYSTEMS

[SOURCES]
____ is the environment / people which the child is not directly involved with but influence by.

(A) ** Exosystem

(B) Microsystem

(C) Mesosystem

(D) Macrosystem

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The exosystem contains environmental elements that have a profound influence on a child’s development, even though that child is not directly involved with them. The exosystem is a part of Urie Bronfenbrenner’s ecological systems theory.

Concept note-2: -Exosystem refers to the indirect environment or the interaction between settings that may not directly impact the child, such as a neighborhood or parent’s workplace.

Concept note-3: -The Exosystem incorporates links between the child’s immediate environment and the social settings in which the child does not have an active role, indirectly affecting development by acting on the child’s Micro-and Mesosystems.

Concept note-4: -Examples of exosystems include the neighborhood, parent’s workplaces, parent’s friends and the mass media. These are environments in which the child is not involved, and are external to their experience, but nonetheless affects them anyway.

Concept note-5: -An exosystem refers to one or more settings that do not involve the developing person as an active participant, but in which events occur that affect-or are affected by-what happens in the setting containing the developing person.
