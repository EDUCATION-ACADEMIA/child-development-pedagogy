If Mendel’s first experiment involved cross-pollinated a true-breeding round pea plant and a true-breeding wrinkled pea plant, what would the genotypes of the parents have to be?

(A) RR x Rr

(B) Rr x Rr

(C) rr x rr

(D) ** RR x rr

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Two pea plants one with round green seeds (RRyy) and another with wrinkled yellow (rrYY) seeds produce F1 progeny that have round, yellow (RrYy) seeds. When F1 plants are selfed, the F2 progeny will have a new combination of characters.

Concept note-2: -Pure-bred round-yellow pea seeds have genotype RRYY and the pure-bred wrinkled-green pea seeds have genotype rryy. Keeping this in mind, write the phenotypes of the following genotypes of hybrid pea seeds.

Concept note-3: -When a tall and short plant are crossed, all of the offspring are tall. If the offspring self-fertilize, they produce tall and short plants in a ratio of 3:1 in the next generation. Mendel’s actual counts were 787 tall:277 short plants in this generation (2.84:1 ratio).
