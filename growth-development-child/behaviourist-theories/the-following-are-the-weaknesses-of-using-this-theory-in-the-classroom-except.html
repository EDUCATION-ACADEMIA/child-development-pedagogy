The following are the weaknesses of using this theory in the classroom EXCEPT

(A) limited

(B) lack of intrinsic motivation

(C) ** lack of extrinsic motivation

(D) behaviour doesn’t show actual understanding

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Why is extrinsic motivation bad? External motivation often creates pressure and, contrary to what you may think, our brain just doesn’t work well under pressure. We become less happy. The brain’s levels of the neurotransmitters serotonin and dopamine decrease.

Concept note-2: -Extrinsic motivation uses rewards or incentives-like praise, fame, or money-as motivation for specific activities. It is based on external factors and may be less effective long-term than intrinsic motivation. Extrinsic motivation is a type of operant conditioning, which is a form of behavior modification.

Concept note-3: -Joining a debate society to gain knowledge is not an example of extrinsic motivation, it is a intrinsic motivation, defining as the doing of an activity for its inherent satisfaction rather than for some separable consequence.
