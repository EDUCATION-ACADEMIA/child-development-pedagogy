Fish can survive in a pond during winter because liquid water remains below the solid ice surface. Which of these describes the property that allows some liquid water to remain in “frozen” lakes?

(A) Water is a polar molecule

(B) ** Water is less dense as a solid than as a liquid

(C) Water has a high level of hardness when frozen

(D) Water holds less air at lower temperatures

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Some species, like koi and gobies, may burrow into soft sediments and go dormant like frogs and other amphibians, but most fish simply school in the deepest pools and take a “winter rest.” In this resting state, fishes’ hearts slow down, their needs for food and oxygen decrease, and they move about very little.

Concept note-2: -Underneath the frozen upper layer, the water remains in its liquid form and do not get the freeze. Oxygen is trapped beneath the layer of ice. As a result, fish and other aquatic animals find it possible to live comfortably in the frozen ponds.

Concept note-3: -Which of these is a property of ice that allows fish to survive in ponds when air temperatures are below 0° C? Ice floats, insulating warmer liquid water below it.

Concept note-4: -The unique salts in the pond lower the freezing point, which is why this saline pond can exist in a place where the temperature ranges from minus 50 to plus 10 degrees Celsius (-58 to +50 F).
