Which of these is the greatest limiting factor for plants that grow on the floor of a rain forest?

(A) Herbivores

(B) ** Sunlight

(C) Water

(D) Space

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Light is the limiting factor for these plants and therefore, they are adapted to survive under low light conditions. Moisture availability and related adaptations are not of consideration. Thus, the correct answer is option B.

Concept note-2: -Sunlight: This is a big limiting factor for tropical rainforests. Plants on the floor don’t get much sunlight because of the leaves in the canopy level of trees so smaller plants have to adapt to survive off of little amounts of sunlight.

Concept note-3: -One of the most important limiting factors for plant growth is water; rain forest plants need more water than savanna plants (and therefore cannot grow on savannas) and savanna plants need more water than desert plants (and therefore cannot naturally be grown in deserts).

Concept note-4: -Sunlight is a major limiting factor. A variety of strategies have been successful in the struggle to reach light or to adapt to the low intensity of light beneath the canopy.

Concept note-5: -Since only two percent of the sunlight can filter through the top layers to the understory, very few plants grow there. ‘’ So, since the forest floor receives almost no sunlight, very few plants grow there.
