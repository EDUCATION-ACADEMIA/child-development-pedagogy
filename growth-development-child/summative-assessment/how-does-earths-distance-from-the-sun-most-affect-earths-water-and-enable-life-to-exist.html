How does Earth’s distance from the Sun most affect Earth’s water and enable life to exist?

(A) ** It permits liquid water

(B) It allows liquid water to freeze

(C) It changes water’s specific gravity

(D) It allows water to be salty or fresh.

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -It regulates the temperature of water bodies, weather patterns and provides energy to the growth of plants. The distance of the Earth from the Sun makes it a perfect reason for the life because it receives the perfect amount of heat and light to allow life to be created and to support it.

Concept note-2: -Organisms can live on Earth because of Earth’s location in the solar system. How does Earth’s distance from the Sun MOST affect Earth’s water and enable life to exist? It permits liquid water.

Concept note-3: -It is the right distance from the Sun, it is protected from harmful solar radiation by its magnetic field, it is kept warm by an insulating atmosphere, and it has the right chemical ingredients for life, including water and carbon.

Concept note-4: -THE distance of the sun from the earth or, speaking more correctly-for the distance is, of course, variable-the semi-major axis of the earth’s orbit, is the most important constant in astronomy. It determines the scale not merely of the solar system but also of the whole universe.

Concept note-5: -The sun has extremely important influences on our planet: It drives weather, ocean currents, seasons, and climate, and makes plant life possible through photosynthesis. Without the sun’s heat and light, life on Earth would not exist.
