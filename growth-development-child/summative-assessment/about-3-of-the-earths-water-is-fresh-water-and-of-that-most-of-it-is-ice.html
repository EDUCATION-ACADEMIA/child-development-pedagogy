SUMMATIVE ASSESSMENT

[SOURCES]
About 3% of the Earth’s water is fresh water, and of that, most of it is ice.

(A) ** true

(B) false

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Saline water in oceans, seas and saline groundwater make up about 97% of all the water on Earth. Only 2.5–2.75% is fresh water, including 1.75–2% frozen in glaciers, ice and snow, 0.5–0.75% as fresh groundwater and soil moisture, and less than 0.01% of it as surface water in lakes, swamps and rivers.

Concept note-2: -Only a little over 3% of Earth’s water is fresh. Most of that fresh water (68.7%) is frozen in glaciers and ice caps.

Concept note-3: -3% of the earth’s water is fresh. 2.5% of the earth’s fresh water is unavailable: locked up in glaciers, polar ice caps, atmosphere, and soil; highly polluted; or lies too far under the earth’s surface to be extracted at an affordable cost. 0.5% of the earth’s water is available fresh water.

Concept note-4: -Rivers and Streams Only about three percent of Earth’s water is freshwater. Of that, only about 1.2 percent can be used as drinking water; the rest is locked up in glaciers, ice caps, and permafrost, or buried deep in the ground.

Concept note-5: -About 2.1% of all of Earth’s water is frozen in glaciers. less than 1% is in all living plants and animals.
