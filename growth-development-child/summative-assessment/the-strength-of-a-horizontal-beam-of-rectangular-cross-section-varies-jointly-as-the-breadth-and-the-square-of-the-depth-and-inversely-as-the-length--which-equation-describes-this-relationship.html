The strength of a horizontal beam of rectangular cross section varies jointly as the breadth and the square of the depth and inversely as the length . Which equation describes this relationship?

(A) L = kb d 2 S

(B) L = S kb d 2

(C) ** S = kb d 2 L

(D) S = L kb d 2

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Answer and Explanation: Given that the strength of a rectangular beam varies jointly as its width (w) and the square of its depth (d), we can express this as: s∝wd2.

Concept note-2: -What actually varies as “the product of width and square of depth” is rigidity. A beam twice as wide and four twice as deep will have one-eight the deflection at the same load.

Concept note-3: -⇒d=√23⋅2r. Q. The strength of a beam varies as the product of its breadth and square of its depth.

Concept note-4: -A beam of rectangular cross-section is subjected to a bending moment M (N·m) and a maximum shear force V (N). The bending stress in the beam is calculated as =6M/bd2 (Pa), and average shear stress is calculated as =3V/2bd (Pa), where b is the width and d is the depth of the beam.
