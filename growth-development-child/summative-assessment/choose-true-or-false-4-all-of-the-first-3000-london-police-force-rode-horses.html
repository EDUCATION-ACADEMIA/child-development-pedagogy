Concept note-1: -The Bow Street Runners were the first professional police force, organised in London by magistrate and author Henry Fielding in 1749.

Concept note-2: -The Metropolitan Police Service (MPS), formerly and still commonly known as the Metropolitan Police (and informally as the Met Police, the Met, Scotland Yard, or the Yard), is the territorial police force responsible for law enforcement and the prevention of crime in Greater London.

Concept note-3: -34, 350 Police officers. 10, 253 Police staff. 1, 196 Police community support officers. 1, 589 Special officers.

Concept note-4: -In 1828, another Commons inquiry reported in favour of a police force for London, and in 1829 Peel’s Metropolitan Police Bill received parliamentary approval. The new Act established a full-time, professional and centrally-organised police force for the greater London area under the control of the Home Secretary.
