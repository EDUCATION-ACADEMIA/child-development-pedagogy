When is momentum conserved?

(A) During inelastic collisions

(B) During elastic collisions

(C) When the collision is inelastic OR elastic but never an explosion.

(D) ** All collisions and explosions.

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Whether it is a collision or an explosion, if it occurs in an isolated system, then each object involved encounters the same impulse to cause the same momentum change. The impulse and momentum change on each object are equal in magnitude and opposite in direction. Thus, the total system momentum is conserved.

Concept note-2: -Momentum is always conserved, regardless of collision type. Mass is conserved regardless of collision type as well, but the mass may be deformed by an inelastic collision, resulting in the two original masses being stuck together.

Concept note-3: -When a collision occurs in an isolated system, the total momentum of the system of objects is conserved. Provided that there are no net external forces acting upon the objects, the momentum of all objects before the collision equals the momentum of all objects after the collision.

Concept note-4: -A system must meet two requirements for its momentum to be conserved: The mass of the system must remain constant during the interaction. As the objects interact (apply forces on each other), they may transfer mass from one to another; but any mass one object gains is balanced by the loss of that mass from another.
