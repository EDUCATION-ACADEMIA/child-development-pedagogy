The momentum of an object depends upon the object’s ____ & ____

(A) size and shape

(B) mass and speed

(C) ** mass and veloctiy

(D) mass and energy

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The momentum of an object depends on both mass and velocity. Heavier products tend to produce a greater force and thus have greater momentum. Momentum also depends on how fast the object is travelling. For example, a faster car will have a greater momentum than a slower one.

Concept note-2: -p = m v . You can see from the equation that momentum is directly proportional to the object’s mass (m) and velocity (v). Therefore, the greater an object’s mass or the greater its velocity, the greater its momentum. A large, fast-moving object has greater momentum than a smaller, slower object.

Concept note-3: -The momentum, p of an object is defined as the product of its mass, m and velocity, V. Momentum = Mass x Velocity. That is, p = mv.

Concept note-4: -Momentum depends on how much mass things have and how fast they are moving. When things crash into each other, momentum is transferred.
