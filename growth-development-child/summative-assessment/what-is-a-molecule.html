What is a molecule?

(A) Two or more atoms of the same element

(B) Two or more atoms of different elements

(C) The basic building block of a compound

(D) ** All the answer choices are correct

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Molecules are made up of one or more atoms. If they contain more than one atom, the atoms can be the same (an oxygen molecule has two oxygen atoms) or different (a water molecule has two hydrogen atoms and one oxygen atom). Biological molecules, such as proteins and DNA, can be made up of many thousands of atoms.

Concept note-2: -A molecule is two or more atoms connected by chemical bonds, which form the smallest unit of a substance that retains the composition and properties of that substance. Molecules form the basis of chemistry. Molecules are noted with the element symbol and a subscript with the number of atoms.

Concept note-3: -A group of two or more than two atoms of the same or different elements that are chemically bonded together is called a molecule. For example, two atoms of hydrogen and one atom of oxygen react with each other and form one molecule of water.

Concept note-4: -Molecules are of three types: Molecule of an atom, Molecule of an element and Molecule of a compound.
