What is a mixture? (Choose all the answers that apply)

(A) ** Multiple substances joined together but not chemically combined

(B) Two atoms of the same element

(C) Two or more atoms of different elements chemically combined

(D) ** Two or more substances that can be physically separated

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -A mixture is a material composed of two or more simpler substances in chemistry. Such materials can be compounds or chemical elements. A mixture of liquids, solids, or gases can be produced. When sugar is put in water, for example, it forms a mixture, then it dissolves to create a solution.

Concept note-2: -A heterogeneous mixture is a mixture of two or more chemical substances (elements or compounds) where the different components can be visually distinguished and easily separated by physical means.

Concept note-3: -If two or more substances (elements or compounds) are mixed together in any proportion, and they do not undergo any chemical change but retain their properties, the resulting substance is called mixture.

Concept note-4: -(a) A mixture is a combination of two or more substances, which do not react with each other. Examples: sugar and water, sugar and sand, etc.
